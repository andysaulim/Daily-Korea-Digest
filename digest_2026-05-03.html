Top Stories

Kim Jong Un urges active youth role in party affairs at Socialist congress

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un addressed the 11th Congress of the Socialist Patriotic Youth League, calling for the young generation to take a more active role in Workers' Party affairs and state development projects. The speech marks intensified youth mobilization efforts as Pyongyang faces labor shortages amid expanded weapons production for Russia and accelerated construction projects including new satellite launch facilities.

So what: Precedes May 5 Youth Day celebrations and potential loyalty demonstrations

Pattern: Youth mobilization drives historically precede major construction pushes, last seen before Pyongyang General Hospital project in 2020

Yonhap

Gov't approves 560 billion won injection into AI startup Upstage

Financial authorities on Sunday approved a 560 billion won government investment in South Korean AI startup Upstage, marking the largest single public funding for an AI company in Korea's history. The injection aims to develop sovereign AI capabilities in Korean language processing and industrial applications as Seoul races to catch up with global leaders in artificial intelligence amid intensifying US-China technology competition.

So what: Advances Korea's sovereign AI initiative announced at Trump-Lee summit

Yonhap

North Korea's Kim casts youth as vanguard of state goals amid Russia war

Kim Jong Un positioned North Korea's youth as the vanguard of achieving state objectives during a major party congress speech, as Pyongyang deepens military cooperation with Russia's war effort. The mobilization drive comes as North Korea reportedly prepares to send additional munitions shipments to Russia through Rason port, with youth brigades increasingly deployed to weapons factories and construction sites supporting the bilateral partnership.

So what: Aligns with expected munitions production surge ahead of summer Russia offensive

North Korea calls US cyber threat claims fabrication, warns countermeasures
North Korea dismissed US allegations of hacking activities as 'absurd slander' through a foreign ministry statement on Sunday and warned of potential countermeasures. The denial follows escalating US accusations of DPRK cyber operations targeting financial institutions and critical infrastructure globally, with recent US Treasury sanctions on alleged North Korean IT workers operating abroad.

Progressive civic group holds protest rally outside US Embassy
A progressive civic group organized a protest demonstration outside the US Embassy in Seoul on May 2, with participants calling for revision of US-Korea alliance terms. The rally, which drew several hundred protesters, reflects ongoing tensions within South Korean civil society regarding military burden-sharing and the presence of US forces.
Business · Yonhap English
Nearly 900 Air Force pilots left for civilian airlines over past decade
Data shows 896 veteran Air Force pilots with 8-17 years experience voluntarily left military service from 2017 to March 2026, with 69.4% joining Korean Air. The exodus of pilots capable of independently carrying out operations and training junior aviators has raised concerns about military readiness amid poor working conditions in the armed forces.
US-Korea · Korea Times
S. Korea takes cautious stance after US tariff hike on EU
South Korea will carefully monitor Trump administration's decision to raise tariffs on EU autos and trucks to 25% from 15%, a Cheong Wa Dae official said Saturday. Seoul has a similar tariff deal with Washington under which the rate was reduced to 15%, raising concerns the US could apply similar increases to Korean exports.
Technology · Yonhap English
S. Korean Earth-observation satellite successfully put into orbit
South Korea's Earth-observation satellite launched from Vandenberg Space Force Base successfully entered orbit and established communication with ground stations on Saturday. The satellite, part of Korea's expanding space surveillance capabilities, will provide high-resolution imagery for environmental monitoring and disaster response operations.
Business · Reuters Korea
Seoul holds 'Power Nap Contest' in sleep-deprived South Korea
Seoul hosted a 'Power Nap Contest' Saturday in response to South Korea's chronic sleep deprivation, with participants competing to fall asleep fastest in a public setting. The event highlights growing awareness of health impacts from Korea's work culture, where employees average just 5.5 hours of sleep nightly according to recent OECD data.
