What changed and why

Structure. The four data structures (tariffs, investment, policy, deals) become three labeled pillars, each leading with its own hero figure — so the eye knows what it's looking at instead of scanning six near-identical tables.

The progress bar is back. "$127B of $350B" as a filled bar conveys "how far along" instantly — a number alone doesn't. This is the piece that was missing.

A plain-language read up top. One sentence of state-of-play before the data, matching how every other section leads.

Full vs. Lean is the open question. Full keeps every table in the email; Lean shows the top 3 and pushes the static reference (full sector rates, all 14 deals) to the web edition for a shorter daily read. Tell me which — or mix (e.g. Lean tariffs, Full investment).
