NK-Russia-China

Kim Jong Un vows 'unshakeable will' to develop ties with China's Xi on CCP's 105th founding anniversary

Kim Jong Un sent a congratulatory message to Xi Jinping marking the 105th anniversary of the founding of the Chinese Communist Party, pledging an 'unshakeable will' to deepen bilateral ties, according to multiple outlets citing KCNA. The message is the first major DPRK diplomatic signal toward Beijing since Kim chaired the Workers' Party plenum on June 23-25 — where he declared North Korea would 'exercise its position as a nuclear state' and pledged full support for Russian forces in Ukraine. The outreach underscores that despite Pyongyang's deepening Russia alignment, Kim is publicly preserving the China relationship.

So what: Watch for Beijing's response signaling whether China views the post-plenum nuclear posture declarations as a diplomatic liability or endorses DPRK's current escalatory trajectory.
