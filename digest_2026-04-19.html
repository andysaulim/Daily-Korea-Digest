Top Stories

North Korea fires multiple short-range ballistic missiles toward East Sea

North Korea launched multiple short-range ballistic missiles from near Pyongyang at 10:25 a.m. Sunday, with projectiles traveling approximately 140 kilometers before falling into the East Sea. South Korea's Joint Chiefs of Staff detected the launches from the Sunan area, prompting immediate coordination with US and Japanese defense officials who confirmed tracking at least three missiles. The Office of National Security condemned the launches as clear violations of UN Security Council resolutions and convened an emergency meeting to assess the security situation.

So what: Complicates President Lee's India-Indonesia diplomatic tour starting today

Pattern: First missile test since Mar 11 cruise missiles, breaking 39-day pause

Yonhap/JCS

North Korea's missile launches flex military muscle amid regional tensions

Washington Post analysis connects North Korea's latest ballistic missile tests to broader regional dynamics including ongoing Iran-Israel tensions and upcoming Trump-Xi summit in May. Defense analysts note Pyongyang's pattern of conducting provocations when global attention focuses elsewhere, with today's launches coinciding with President Lee's departure for India and Indonesia. The 140-kilometer flight path suggests testing of close-range strike capabilities targeting US and ROK forces stationed near Seoul, while demonstrating continued weapons development despite international sanctions.

Washington Post

Turkish troops in South Korea: 1962 archives reveal NATO's forgotten mission

Newly released archives from 1962 reveal Turkish military forces maintained a 5,000-strong brigade in South Korea as part of UN Command, highlighting NATO's early involvement in Korean Peninsula security that predates current discussions about European participation. The Turkish Brigade, which arrived during the Korean War and remained until 1971, conducted joint patrols along the DMZ and participated in early US-ROK combined exercises, establishing precedents for multinational force integration. Historical documents show Turkish forces suffered 3,500 casualties defending Seoul during the war, creating lasting military ties that influence modern Turkey-South Korea defense industry cooperation worth $2.5 billion annually.

Lee departs for India on two-nation Asia swing
President Lee Jae-myung departed Seoul for New Delhi on his first bilateral state visit since taking office in February, seeking to strengthen defense ties and economic partnerships with India. The four-day India tour includes meetings with PM Modi, business roundtables focused on defense cooperation and infrastructure deals worth an estimated $5 billion, and discussions on semiconductor supply chain cooperation. The trip extends to Indonesia for ASEAN cooperation talks and Korean cultural exchanges, marking Lee's most ambitious diplomatic outreach aimed at diversifying Korea's strategic partnerships beyond traditional allies.
Business · CNA Korea
Seoul, Washington agree won's excessive volatility undesirable
South Korean and US officials agreed the Korean won's excessive volatility is not desirable amid ongoing currency market pressures, with the won trading near 1,465 per dollar. Finance ministry officials from both countries held emergency consultations as the won weakened 8% year-to-date, raising concerns about imported inflation and financial stability. The coordination signals potential intervention if volatility increases further, with both governments prepared to take stabilizing measures including possible coordinated currency market action.

DPRK · CNN Korea
North Korean brothers spent 10 years planning escape to freedom
Two North Korean brothers who spent a decade planning their defection finally reached South Korea after a harrowing journey through China and Southeast Asia, revealing the extensive underground networks facilitating escapes. The brothers, now in their 30s, saved money by trading illegally and studied Chinese for years before attempting their crossing, highlighting the sophisticated planning required for successful defections. Their story emerges as Seoul reports defector numbers dropped 70% since COVID border closures, with only 196 North Koreans reaching the South in 2025 compared to over 1,000 annually before the pandemic.
Technology · Korea Economic Daily
Korean blockchain games make fortune overseas as domestic rules choke P2E
South Korean blockchain gaming companies generated $2.3 billion in overseas revenue during 2025 while being effectively banned from operating domestically due to strict gaming regulations prohibiting play-to-earn mechanics. Major Korean developers including Wemade, Com2uS, and Netmarble have established subsidiaries in Singapore and Dubai to serve global markets, with their games ranking in top 10 blockchain gaming charts worldwide. The regulatory disconnect highlights Korea's struggle to balance consumer protection with emerging technology leadership, as domestic players miss out on a market projected to reach $65 billion globally by 2027.
