SK Hynix raises $26.5B in US share sale — largest-ever US IPO by a foreign firm

SK Hynix priced a $26.5 billion US share offering, the largest US IPO ever completed by a foreign company, with the offering guided at 3.1% above its Korea close, per Bloomberg. The listing comes as US Commerce Secretary Lutnick separately urged both Samsung and SK Hynix to expand memory chip production in the United States. The IPO injects major Korean semiconductor capital into US markets while governance questions have been raised in Seoul over the company's government-linked $732 billion domestic investment plan.

So what — The IPO and Lutnick's production push together frame the near-term US-Korea semiconductor supply chain agenda ahead of Section 122 expiry on Jul 24.

Xi meets DPRK premier in Beijing as North Korea formally announces nuclear expansion

Chinese President Xi Jinping met North Korea's premier in Beijing ahead of the 65th anniversary of the China-DPRK Treaty of Friendship, Cooperation, and Mutual Assistance, per Yonhap and Reuters. The summit-level contact occurs on the same day KCNA formally announced DPRK decisions to expand nuclear forces, presenting Beijing with the challenge of managing its treaty ally's escalating posture while maintaining its own strategic interests. Xi's July 6 statement pledging 'long-term, stable' China-DPRK ties frames the diplomatic context for the premier's visit.

So what — The Xi-DPRK premier meeting is the highest-level China-North Korea contact since the June 25 plenum and directly precedes the July 27 armistice anniversary — a historically sensitive escalation window.
