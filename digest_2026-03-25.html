Top Stories

KF-21 fighter jet begins mass production

Korea rolled out its first mass-produced KF-21 Boramae fighter jet with President Lee Jae Myung attending the ceremony in Sacheon, marking the culmination of a 25-year development program. The domestically developed jet represents a major milestone in Korea's defense industry self-reliance efforts. Korea Aerospace Industries led the project alongside Hanwha Aerospace, Hanwha Systems, the Air Force and the Agency for Defense Development.

So what: Directly supports Korea's ambition to become a top-4 global defense powerhouse and compete for international fighter jet export contracts.

Korea Times

Korean sub to make trans-Pacific journey for joint drills with Canada

The ROKS Dosan Ahn Chang-ho will travel 14,000 kilometers across the Pacific for the first time to participate in joint drills with Canada in June, with stops in Guam and Hawaii. The 3,000-ton Korean naval submarine deployment comes as a Korean consortium competes with Germany's Thyssenkrupp Marine Systems for a landmark contract to supply 12 submarines to the Canadian navy. Two Canadian submariners will join the Korean crew for the final leg from Hawaii to Victoria.

So what: Directly supports Korea's bid for the Canadian submarine contract valued at approximately 60 trillion won ($40 billion).

GM to invest $600 million in Korea to strengthen nation as global hub for small SUVs

General Motors announced plans to invest $600 million in South Korea to strengthen the country as a global manufacturing hub for small SUVs. The investment will modernize GM Korea's facilities and support the company's global compact vehicle strategy. GM Korea produces vehicles for both domestic and export markets across Asia-Pacific.

So what: Reinforces Korea's position in global automotive supply chains amid ongoing US-Korea strategic investment cooperation.

US-Korea · Korea Times
Korea repatriates drug kingpin from Philippines
Korea successfully repatriated imprisoned drug lord Park Wang-yeol from the Philippines, less than a month after President Lee Jae Myung requested temporary custody during a bilateral summit. The repatriation reflects diplomatic progress after more than nine years of stalemate in extradition proceedings. Park faces investigation for allegedly continuing to smuggle drugs into Korea via Telegram while serving a 60-year sentence in the Philippines for multiple murders.
