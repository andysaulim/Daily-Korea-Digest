Former President Yoon sentenced to 30 years for drone infiltration

Seoul Central District Court sentenced former President Yoon to 30 years in prison for authorizing military drone flights over Pyongyang that scattered propaganda leaflets. The court ruled Yoon abused presidential authority and endangered national security by ordering the missions without cabinet approval. The verdict marks the harshest criminal sentence for a former South Korean president since military dictator Chun Doo-hwan received life imprisonment in 1996.

So what: Verdict sets precedent for prosecuting security policy decisions by former presidents

SK Hynix to list on Nasdaq in up to $50B offering

SK Hynix selected Nasdaq over NYSE for its planned U.S. secondary listing that could value the memory chip maker at up to $50 billion, according to sources familiar with the matter. The world's second-largest memory chip producer aims to complete the listing by year-end, capitalizing on surging demand for high-bandwidth memory chips used in AI applications. The listing would mark the largest Korean tech IPO in the U.S. market and provide SK Hynix with dollar funding for capacity expansion.

So what: Listing provides SK Hynix direct access to U.S. capital markets amid semiconductor competition

US-Korea

Military grounds entire Miron helicopter fleet over engine defects

South Korea's military suspended all LAH-1 Miron helicopter operations after discovering engine corrosion in 47 of 57 aircraft assembled by Hanwha Aerospace. Defense procurement officials found cracks in diffuser components of 38 helicopters' engines, originally developed by French firm Safran. The grounding affects Korea's indigenous light armed helicopter program worth 1.2 trillion won ($790 million), raising questions about quality control in domestic defense manufacturing.

So what: Fleet grounding disrupts Army aviation operations and delays indigenous helicopter program

Pattern: Similar quality issues plagued K2 tank power pack development, delaying deployment by years
