Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi lands in Pyongyang for two-day North Korea visit

Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi arrived in Pyongyang on Thursday for a two-day visit, marking the first trip by China's top diplomat to North Korea since 2019. The visit comes amid heightened tensions over North Korea's weapons tests and deepening military cooperation with Russia, with Wang expected to meet senior North Korean officials to discuss bilateral relations and regional security issues.

So what: Precedes the May 14-15 Trump-Xi summit in Beijing where North Korea policy will be discussed

AP News

Bank of Korea Keeps Rates Unchanged as Mideast Uncertainty Persists

The Bank of Korea held its key interest rate at 2.5% for a seventh consecutive meeting on Thursday, citing persistent inflation risks from Middle East tensions and elevated oil prices at $95.96 per barrel. Governor Rhee Chang-yong warned that the Iran conflict's impact on energy costs could push inflation above the bank's 2% target through 2026, forcing the central bank to maintain its restrictive stance despite slowing growth projections of 1.7% for the year.

So what: Next BOK rate decision scheduled for April 24 meeting

WSJ

Korea calls in Japanese Embassy official over renewed territorial claim to Dokdo

South Korea's foreign ministry summoned a senior Japanese embassy official on Friday to protest Tokyo's renewed territorial claim to Dokdo in its annual Diplomatic Bluebook, with Director General Kim Sang-hoon lodging a formal complaint to Deputy Chief of Mission Hirotaka Matsuo. Japan's foreign ministry document, submitted to cabinet by Foreign Minister Toshimitsu Motegi, claimed Dokdo as Japanese territory and accused Korea of continuing 'illegal occupation' of the islets by stationing forces there, marking another friction point in bilateral relations despite recent cooperation efforts.

Business · CNBC
South Korea holds rates steady as Iran war fans inflation, growth risks
The Bank of Korea's monetary policy committee voted unanimously to maintain the base rate at 2.5%, extending its pause since October 2025 as policymakers balance slowing economic growth against inflation pressures from the Middle East crisis. Central bank officials highlighted that consumer price inflation accelerated to 2.8% in March from 2.3% in February, driven primarily by energy costs as Brent crude spiked above $95 following Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz to Western vessels.

South Korea President Lee to hold summit with Polish PM Tusk on April 13, media reports say
President Lee Jae Myung will meet Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk in Warsaw on Sunday for talks focused on defense industry cooperation and supply chain partnerships, according to Korean media reports citing presidential office sources. The summit comes as Poland continues its military modernization with Korean defense systems, having already ordered K2 tanks and K9 howitzers worth over $13 billion, with negotiations underway for additional FA-50 fighter jets and Chunmoo multiple rocket launchers.
Japan-Korea · Korea Times
Yakuza member arrested for smuggling 636 kg of marijuana into South Korea
A 53-year-old ethnic Korean yakuza member has been arrested and indicted for smuggling 636 kilograms of marijuana into South Korea, enough for 1.27 million people to smoke at once, in what authorities called the largest-ever narcotics seizure for domestic distribution. The pan-government joint investigation unit on drug crimes said the suspect loaded the drugs into a shipping container from Thailand, with authorities also identifying four Vietnamese nationals who allegedly sold the drugs and planning to request Interpol red notices for their arrest.
NK-Russia-China · AP News
North Korea and China agree to deepen cooperation in talks between foreign ministers
Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi told North Korean officials that Pyongyang is making significant achievements in economic construction despite 'oppression' from hostile forces, as the two countries agreed to strengthen strategic cooperation during Wang's first visit since 2019. The talks come as North Korea faces international pressure over its weapons programs and military support for Russia, with China seeking to reassert influence over its traditional ally ahead of potential U.S.-North Korea diplomatic engagement.
Business · Korea Times
Seoul shifts work hours for 282,000 seniors to ease rush hour
The Ministry of Health and Welfare announced Thursday it will shift work schedules for 282,000 elderly public workers to reduce transit congestion amid the U.S.-Iran oil crisis, moving morning shifts from 9 a.m.-noon to 10 a.m.-1 p.m. and afternoon shifts from 2-5 p.m. to 1-4 p.m. The adjustment follows public backlash over presidential remarks about limiting seniors' free transit rides during rush hour, with the trial program operating throughout April and potentially extending if oil prices remain elevated above $95 per barrel.
