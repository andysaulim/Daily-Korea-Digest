Top Stories
Japan-Korea

ROK-Japan defense ministers agree on AI cooperation, reaffirm denuclearization in Seoul talks

Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back and Japanese Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi concluded talks in Seoul on June 28 — Koizumi's first visit to Seoul as defense minister — agreeing to continue defense exchanges including in AI technology and issuing a joint press statement reaffirming denuclearization of the Korean Peninsula. The meeting came less than a month after the two ministers spoke on the sidelines of the Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore, and reciprocates Ahn's January visit to Tokyo. Earlier this month, the two countries resumed a previously suspended bilateral defense activity.

So what: Directly advances the Camp David trilateral framework's defense cooperation pillar; the AI-cooperation language is a new specific deliverable emerging from this bilateral channel.

China-Korea

Seoul protests to Beijing and Moscow after ~10 Chinese and Russian aircraft violate KADIZ

The ROK government lodged a strong protest with China and Russia on June 28 after approximately ten Chinese and Russian military aircraft entered the Korean Air Defense Identification Zone (KADIZ) without prior notification. The joint incursion — occurring in the days following Kim Jong Un's June 26-27 weapons tests and 'destructive military posture' directive — prompted Seoul to summon diplomatic representatives from both countries. The 매일경제 reported the figure as approximately ten aircraft.

So what: The joint China-Russia KADIZ entry comes amid the most intensive DPRK weapons testing week since at least March 2026 and raises questions about coordination timing.

Technology

Samsung and SK hynix to announce combined ~$650B domestic investment plans at Cheong Wa Dae Monday

Samsung Electronics Chairman Lee Jae-yong and SK Group Chairman Chey Tae-won will attend a 2 p.m. Monday briefing at Cheong Wa Dae, presided over by President Lee Jae-myung, to unveil major investment plans as part of the government's three mega-projects for balanced regional development. Industry watchers expect the two chipmakers to announce combined investment exceeding 1,000 trillion won ($650 billion) over the next ten years. The initiative is jointly led by the trade, science, transport, and energy ministries.

So what: The Monday announcement will set domestic semiconductor investment baseline figures that the ROK government will reference in ongoing US-Korea trade and technology negotiations, including Section 301 discussions and CHIPS Act implementation.
