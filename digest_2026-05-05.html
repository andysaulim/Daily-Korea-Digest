Top Stories

Trump urges South Korea to join Strait of Hormuz mission after vessel fire

Trump said Iran fired at South Korean-operated vessel HMM Namu in Strait of Hormuz and called on Seoul to join US-led 'Project Freedom' mission to protect shipping. The Panama-flagged bulk carrier caught fire after explosion Monday night; crew extinguished blaze with no injuries reported. Seoul is now assessing participation in the Hormuz navigation protection initiative.

So what: Decision on Project Freedom participation will be key agenda item for upcoming US-Korea security consultations

Reuters

North Korean women's soccer team returns to South Korea after 8-year absence

North Korean women's soccer team will play in tournament in South Korea for first time since 2018, marking potential thaw in inter-Korean sports exchanges. The team's participation comes despite continued tensions over nuclear weapons and military cooperation with Russia. Sports diplomacy historically served as precursor to broader diplomatic engagement between the Koreas.

Washington Post

Defense ministry dismisses three Army officers over martial law involvement

Defense ministry dismissed three Army officers and removed another from service over roles in former President Yoon Suk Yeol's December 3, 2024 martial law declaration. Brig. Gen. Kim Jeong-geun, Col. An Mu-seong, and Col. Kim Se-un received highest disciplinary punishment with 50% retirement benefit cuts. The actions conclude military disciplinary reviews of martial law participants.
