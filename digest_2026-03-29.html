Top Stories

Kim Jong Un oversees ground test of high-thrust solid-fuel missile engine

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un supervised a ground test of a newly upgraded high-thrust solid-fuel engine used in long-range missiles, state media reported Sunday. KCNA claimed the engine showed higher thrust capabilities compared to previous versions and is designed for missiles capable of targeting the US mainland. Kim also inspected tests of the Korean People's Army's main battle tank and special operations unit drills.

So what: Directly relevant to upcoming Apr 15 Kim Il Sung birthday celebrations when North Korea traditionally conducts military displays

Pattern: Echoes the March 29, 2016 SRBM test from near Wonsan, marking exactly 10 years between missile-related provocations on this date

Yonhap English

Seoul co-sponsors UN resolution on North Korea human rights

South Korea joined as a co-sponsor of this year's UN resolution condemning North Korea's human rights violations, marking a policy shift under President Lee Jae-myung. The move reverses Seoul's previous abstention stance and signals stronger international pressure on Pyongyang over rights abuses. The resolution will be submitted to the UN General Assembly for consideration.

So what: Affects Seoul's inter-Korean engagement strategy and could complicate future diplomatic outreach to Pyongyang

Yonhap English

Korea weighs inheritance tax based on book value, not market prices

South Korea is considering reforming its inheritance tax system to use book values instead of market prices for asset valuation, potentially reducing tax burdens on wealthy families. The proposal could significantly impact chaebol succession planning and family wealth transfers. The change would affect how conglomerate shares and real estate holdings are valued for tax purposes.
