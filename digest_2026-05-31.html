US-Korea

Defense minister seeks U.S. congressional support for S. Korea's nuclear-powered sub project

Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back sought bipartisan U.S. congressional support for South Korea's nuclear-powered submarine project during meetings with House and Senate members at the Shangri-La Dialogue. Ahn met separately with Representatives Pat Harrigan, Gregory Meeks, and Michael Baumgartner, plus Senators Tammy Duckworth and Pete Ricketts, seeking backing for nuclear submarine development, wartime operational control transfer, and shipbuilding cooperation. The meetings mark Seoul's first detailed congressional outreach since Trump granted initial approval for the nuclear submarine program in October 2025.

So what: Directly affects Congressional budget authorization for the nuclear submarine technology transfer

Japan-Korea

S. Korea discussed bilateral military logistics support pact with Japan: defense minister

South Korea discussed a Japan-proposed bilateral military logistics support pact with Tokyo for the first time, though Seoul maintains reservations about the Acquisition and Cross-Servicing Agreement (ACSA). Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back acknowledged the discussions following bilateral talks with Japanese Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi at the Shangri-La Dialogue. Seoul expressed caution about the agreement, citing the need for understanding and persuasion from both countries' populations before proceeding.

So what: First official confirmation that Seoul is considering deeper Japan defense cooperation beyond current intelligence-sharing arrangements

Business · Korea Times
Gov't denies foreign worker scheme aims to expand cheap labor
The Ministry of Justice rejected concerns that its new regional vitality special employment scheme for depopulated areas aims to expand access to cheap migrant labor. The program allows small businesses and agricultural corporations in designated areas to hire F-2-R visa holders for long-term work. Officials insisted the goal is addressing structural labor shortages in depopulation areas rather than simple cost-cutting through low-paid foreign workers.
Energy · Yonhap English
6th S. Korean oil tanker successfully transits through Red Sea
A South Korean oil tanker successfully transited the Red Sea en route home after loading crude oil at Saudi Arabia's Yanbu Port, marking the sixth shipment to use the route. The vessel passed through the Red Sea at around 10 a.m. Korea time, with movement details undisclosed for safety reasons. Korea began diverting crude shipments away from the Strait of Hormuz due to Iran's effective blockade amid its conflict with the United States.
