Business

Samsung Biologics to acquire Swiss-based CDMO firm for 2.7 trillion won

Samsung Biologics announced Monday it will acquire a Swiss-based contract drug manufacturing organization for 2.7 trillion won, a major expansion of its global pharmaceutical manufacturing footprint. The deal follows Samsung Biologics' earlier $280M acquisition of GSK's Rockville, Maryland facility, which is tracked on the White House US-Korea investment list. The Swiss acquisition extends Samsung Biologics' CDMO capacity into European markets.

So what — Expands Samsung Biologics' global CDMO footprint; the pharma sector is one where the US-Korea deal caps Section 232 tariffs at up to 15% — monitoring whether this acquisition affects US regulatory posture.
