Seoul stocks soar nearly 3% amid hopes for Hormuz Strait reopening

South Korean stocks jumped 2.94% Friday on hopes that diplomatic efforts could ease tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, with the KOSPI closing at 5,377.30 points. The won also strengthened against the dollar to 1,509.92. Heavy oil-dependent companies led gains as France and South Korea announced cooperation on ensuring safe passage through the critical shipping lane.

S. Korea, France agree to cooperate on safe passage through Strait of Hormuz
President Lee Jae Myung and French President Emmanuel Macron agreed to cooperate on ensuring safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz, which has seen increased tensions amid the Middle East conflict. The two leaders held a summit in Seoul as part of Macron's state visit, the first by a European leader to Lee's administration. They also discussed strengthening bilateral defense cooperation and technology partnerships.
NK-Russia-China · NK News
North Korea to bury soldiers from Ukraine war at new cemetery in mid-April
Kim Jong Un visited a new cemetery and museum commemorating North Korean troops who died fighting alongside Russia in Ukraine, with state media reporting burials will take place in mid-April. The visit marks the first official acknowledgment of North Korean casualties in the conflict, though specific numbers were not disclosed. KCNA reports highlighted Kim's attention to 'ever-growing material' demands of the military amid the deployment.
Business · Korea Times
Seoul mayoral primaries heat up as parties field three contenders each
The Democratic Party and People Power Party are each running three candidates in their Seoul mayoral primaries ahead of June 3 local elections. Recent polls show DPK candidates leading potential PPP nominees, with the gap widening compared to earlier surveys. DPK's Chong Won-o brings experience from three consecutive terms as Seongdong District head, while both parties work to finalize their candidates.
DPRK · NK News
ROK police investigate if body in Imjin River came from North Korea
South Korean police are investigating whether a dead body found in the Imjin River near Paju came from North Korea. The body was discovered at 9:20 a.m. Thursday along the inter-Korean border area. Authorities have not disclosed additional details about the body's condition or potential identity as the investigation continues.
US-Korea · Yonhap English
S. Korea advances deployment of Iron Dome-like interceptor to 2029
South Korea will accelerate deployment of its Low-Altitude Missile Defense (LAMD) system by two years to 2029 to better counter North Korean long-range artillery threats. The Defense Acquisition Program Administration approved the timeline at a pan-government committee meeting. The government plans to invest 842 billion won in the system designed to intercept simultaneous attacks at low altitudes.
US-Korea · CNBC Korea
South Korea, France deepen defense cooperation amid Middle East conflict
South Korea and France agreed to strengthen defense cooperation during Macron's state visit to Seoul, with specific focus on ensuring maritime security in the Middle East. The agreement comes as tensions in the Strait of Hormuz threaten global energy supplies. Both nations signed a preliminary MOU on support for Korean War veterans and discussed expanding cooperation in defense industries.
