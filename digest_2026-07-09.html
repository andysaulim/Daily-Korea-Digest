US-Korea

Supreme Court upholds 7-year prison term for ex-President Yoon on obstruction charges

South Korea's Supreme Court upheld a seven-year prison sentence for former President Yoon Suk Yeol for obstructing justice by blocking investigators attempting to detain him following his December 3, 2024 martial law declaration. Thursday's ruling is the first Supreme Court verdict against Yoon across his eight ongoing trials; his main insurrection case is still before an appellate court after a lower court sentenced him to life imprisonment. Seoul Central District Court also sentenced former PSS chief Park Jong-joon to four years and former deputy chief Kim Seong-hoon to five years for physically blocking the arrest warrant execution in January 2025.

So what — The insurrection trial at the appellate level — with a life sentence from the lower court still under review — is the decisive proceeding for Yoon's ultimate legal fate.

NK-Russia-China

DPRK Premier Pak Thae-song to visit China for 65th anniversary of DPRK-China friendship treaty

North Korean Premier Pak Thae-song will make an official visit to China to participate in events marking the 65th anniversary of the signing of the DPRK-China Treaty of Friendship, Cooperation and Mutual Assistance, per KCNA. The visit follows Kim Jong Un's July 1-2 congratulatory message to Xi Jinping on the 105th CCP founding anniversary pledging 'unshakeable will' to deepen bilateral ties, and Xi's June statement expressing readiness for 'long-term, stable' China-DPRK development. Pak's delegation represents the highest-level Pyongyang official visit to Beijing in recent months, occurring as DPRK executes its naval expansion program and as Kim pledged full support for Russian forces in Ukraine at the June 25 Workers' Party plenum.

So what — The treaty anniversary visit will be watched for any joint statement language on the Korean Peninsula security situation, particularly in the context of DPRK's declared nuclear state posture from the June 25 plenum.

Bank of Korea Governor said Thursday the BOK sees a need for a rate hike at an appropriate time, a shift from the central bank's recent posture, with the BOK policy rate currently at 2.50% following the July 2026 change.
