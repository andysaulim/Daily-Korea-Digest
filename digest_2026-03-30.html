Top Stories

Seoul stocks tumble 3.35% on Middle East war escalation fears

KOSPI plummeted 3.35% to close at 5,277.30 as investors fled risk assets amid intensifying Middle East conflict, marking the index's sharpest single-day decline since February 2026. Heavy selling concentrated in semiconductor and battery stocks with Samsung Electronics down 4.2% and SK Hynix falling 5.1% on concerns over disrupted supply chains and surging energy costs. Foreign investors net sold 1.2 trillion won while the Korean won weakened past 1,515 per dollar as safe-haven demand for US currency intensified.

So what: Market rout complicates BOK's April 24 rate decision calculus between inflation pressures and growth support.

Yonhap · Mar 30

Finance ministry reviews 10 trillion won emergency financing amid crisis

Finance ministry and Korea Eximbank initiated review of 10 trillion won policy financing package to support exporters facing Middle East supply chain disruptions and payment delays. Emergency measures include expanded trade insurance coverage, working capital loans at preferential rates, and fast-track approvals for affected companies in energy, construction and shipping sectors. Review comes as 60 Korean workers evacuated from Iraq facilities and multiple infrastructure projects face force majeure declarations.

So what: Signals government preparing for prolonged Middle East crisis impact on Korea's export-dependent economy.

Yonhap · Mar 30

Satellite imagery reveals North Korea disguising missile site as tree farm

NK Pro analysis of commercial satellite imagery shows large-scale construction at Korean People's Army training base designed to mimic civilian tree nursery infrastructure. Pattern of buildings, access roads and security perimeter matches known military-run agricultural facilities used for dual-purpose concealment of weapons programs. Discovery suggests systematic effort to hide military assets behind agricultural facade as international monitoring of DPRK sites intensifies.

So what: Complicates verification efforts ahead of any future denuclearization negotiations.

NK Pro · Mar 30

Government targets 800 trillion won 2027 budget for AI transformation

Cabinet approved 2027 fiscal framework potentially reaching 800 trillion won with unprecedented focus on artificial intelligence infrastructure and digital transformation. Budget marks 12% increase from 2026 levels with new allocations for AI compute clusters, quantum research facilities, and nationwide 6G testbeds. Finance ministry projects AI investments could add 2.3% to GDP growth by 2030 through productivity gains across manufacturing and services.

So what: Largest peacetime budget expansion signals all-in bet on AI competitiveness race with China.

China's flag carrier resumes Beijing-Pyongyang direct flights after years
Air China resumed regular passenger service between Beijing and Pyongyang, marking the restoration of direct air links suspended since the COVID-19 pandemic began. The twice-weekly service using Boeing 737 aircraft signals normalization of DPRK-China people-to-people exchanges after years of strict border controls. Flight resumption follows the March 12 restart of cross-border train service between Sinuiju and Dandong, indicating accelerated reopening of North Korea's China border.
Energy · Yonhap English
LG Chem secures 27,000 tons of Russian naphtha amid sanctions
LG Chem confirmed acquisition of 27,000 tons of Russian naphtha feedstock for petrochemical production, navigating complex sanctions compliance requirements. The purchase comes as Korean chemical companies face supply pressures from Middle East disruptions while maintaining cautious approach to Russian materials. Deal structure reportedly uses third-country intermediaries and non-sanctioned payment mechanisms to ensure compliance with US secondary sanctions framework.
Energy · Yonhap English
President Lee says energy crisis sparked by Middle East war keeps him awake
President Lee Jae-myung expressed deep concern over energy security implications of escalating Middle East conflict, stating the crisis causes him sleepless nights worrying about Korea's energy vulnerability. With Korea importing over 95% of energy needs and Middle East supplying 70% of crude oil, the war threatens fundamental economic stability. Lee ordered emergency stockpile reviews and accelerated renewable energy deployment to reduce dependency on volatile Middle East supplies.

Labor union chief challenges President's employment flexibility push
Korean Federation of Trade Unions chairman criticized President Lee's employment flexibility proposals, arguing proper safety nets must be established before loosening job protections. Union position reflects growing labor concerns over potential weakening of employment security amid economic pressures from Middle East crisis. Statement signals potential friction between Lee administration's economic reform agenda and traditional Democratic Party labor constituency ahead of June local elections.
