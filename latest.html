Top Stories

Kim Jong Un formally calls South Korea 'most hostile' nation in landmark speech

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un formally designated South Korea as the 'most hostile nation' in his policy speech at the 15th Supreme People's Assembly on March 21-22. Kim declared that North Korea's status as a nuclear weapons state will never change, marking the first time both positions were enshrined in a major state address. The speech represents the doctrinal completion of Kim's January 2024 pivot away from reunification.

Pattern: Echoes the March 24, 2022 ICBM test that flew 1,090 km at 6,248 km altitude, after which Kim first began shifting rhetoric on the South.

Seoul shares crash 6.2% as Iran crisis triggers worst selloff of 2026

The KOSPI plummeted 6.2% to close at 5,405.75 on March 23, marking its steepest single-day drop this year as the US-Iran military confrontation intensified. Foreign investors dumped a net 1.2 trillion won worth of Korean stocks while the won hit a fresh 17-year low of 1,486.30 per dollar. Samsung Electronics fell 7.8% and SK Hynix dropped 8.2% as semiconductor stocks led the rout.

Yonhap English

Putin congratulates Kim on reelection, signals deeper military-technical ties

Russian President Vladimir Putin congratulated Kim Jong Un on his reelection as DPRK president of state affairs, expressing hope for expanded cooperation in defense and technology sectors. The message, delivered through diplomatic channels on March 23, emphasized Russia's commitment to implementing bilateral agreements reached during their September 2023 summit in Vostochny.
