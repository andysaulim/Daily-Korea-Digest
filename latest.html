Top Stories

North Korea conducts engine test for missile capable of targeting US mainland

Kim Jong Un supervised ground testing of a high-thrust solid-fuel rocket engine that could power intercontinental ballistic missiles targeting the US mainland, state media reported Monday. The test demonstrates continued advancement in DPRK missile technology despite international sanctions. KCNA described the engine as capable of powering strategic weapons systems.

So what: Engine tests historically precede full missile launches by 3-6 months

Pattern: Follows March 2014 NLL military exercises that also prompted artillery exchanges

WaPo Korea

China resumes direct flights to North Korea after 6 years

China's national carrier resumed direct flights between Beijing and Pyongyang after a six-year suspension, marking significant normalization of China-DPRK relations. The resumption follows gradual border reopening and increased bilateral trade since COVID-19 restrictions ended. Flight resumption occurs alongside broader Chinese re-engagement with North Korea.

WaPo Korea

60 Korean workers evacuate from Iraq amid war in Middle East

Sixty Korean corporate workers based in Iraq evacuated to neighboring countries including Kuwait, Turkey and Jordan between March 18 and Sunday with Korean Embassy assistance. An estimated 1,500 Koreans have evacuated the Middle East region since the conflict erupted, with three more leaving Lebanon for Turkey last Friday.

Korean won weakens further against U.S. dollar as Middle East conflict escalates
The won fell to 1,517.08 per dollar Monday as escalating Middle East tensions triggered flight to safe-haven assets. The currency weakness reflects broader market concerns over Korea's energy security and economic exposure to regional conflicts.

Rival parties agree to pass extra budget bill by April 10
The ruling Democratic Party and opposition People Power Party agreed to pass a 25 trillion won extra budget by April 10 to address Middle East war impacts. The extraordinary National Assembly session begins Friday with budget deliberation scheduled for the special parliamentary committee.

President to hold summits with Indonesian, French leaders in Seoul
Lee Jae-myung will meet Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto Tuesday-Thursday and French President Emmanuel Macron Thursday-Friday on state visits. The Indonesian summit will focus on KF-21 fighter jet exports, with Indonesia expected to purchase 16 aircraft as the first international customer.

Korea delays shutdown of coal-powered plants amid energy crisis
The Ministry of Climate, Energy and Environment will extend operations of three coal-fired power stations scheduled to close this year as the government scrambles to secure electricity supplies. President Lee instructed the ministry to reconsider the shutdown plan citing energy security concerns amid Middle East conflict.
