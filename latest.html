Top Stories
Business

Samsung Electronics union launches strike Thursday after wage talks collapse

Samsung Electronics' largest labor union will launch a strike Thursday after last-minute wage negotiations mediated by Labor Minister Kim Young-hoon failed to reach agreement. The National Samsung Electronics Union represents about 28,000 workers and threatened the action over compensation disputes. The Cheong Wa Dae expressed deep regret over the collapse and urged continued efforts to resolve the dispute.

So what: Could disrupt global semiconductor supply chains and complicate Samsung's $6.4B CHIPS Act commitments

Technology

Samsung Electro-Mechanics secures $1.1B AI chip component deal in United States

Samsung Electro-Mechanics signed a 1.5 trillion won ($1.1 billion) contract to supply AI chip components to a US customer, marking one of the largest single component deals for a Korean supplier. The deal reflects surging global demand for AI infrastructure components as data centers expand rapidly. The contract spans multiple years and covers advanced packaging substrates for high-performance computing applications.

So what: Reinforces Korea's position in AI supply chain and supports US semiconductor manufacturing buildout

US-Korea

President Lee criticizes Israeli seizure of Gaza aid ships carrying Korean activists

President Lee Jae-myung condemned Israeli forces for seizing humanitarian aid flotillas bound for Gaza that included Korean activists aboard. The criticism comes as Israeli military operations have intercepted multiple aid convoys in recent days. Lee's statement represents one of the strongest South Korean government positions on the Gaza conflict since the current administration took office.

So what: Reflects Seoul's evolving Middle East diplomacy as regional conflicts affect Korean citizens abroad

North Korea's Naegohyang defeats Suwon FC Women 2-1 in historic inter-Korean match
Naegohyang Women's FC of North Korea defeated South Korea's Suwon FC Women 2-1 in a rare inter-Korean football match held in Suwon despite heavy rain. The match drew significant crowds with some spectators showing support for the North Korean team, causing disappointment for the home team's coach. The event represents a rare moment of sports diplomacy between the divided Koreas.
Energy · Yonhap English
South Korea-operated vessel transits Strait of Hormuz for first time since US-Iran war
A South Korean shipping company vessel successfully passed through the Strait of Hormuz, marking the first transit by a Korean-operated ship since the US-Iran conflict began in February. The Foreign Ministry confirmed the passage as shipping routes gradually reopen after months of disruption. This signals potential normalization of critical energy and trade corridors.
