Top Stories

North Korea says summit with Japan is off unless Tokyo drops 'anachronistic' ways

Kim Yo Jong, sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, rejected Japan's proposal for a summit Monday, demanding Tokyo abandon its "anachronistic" insistence on resolving the abductions issue. In a statement carried by KCNA, Kim said there is "no reason" for North Korea to engage with Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's government while it maintains preconditions. The rejection came after Takaichi broached the summit idea during her recent visit to the United States.

So what: On the agenda for the Mar 28 Quad foreign ministers meeting

South Korean shares tumble, won hits 17-year low on Mideast conflict

The KOSPI index crashed 6.2% Monday, its worst single-day drop since the pandemic, as escalating US-Iran tensions triggered massive foreign selling of Korean equities. The Korean won plunged to 1,486.31 per dollar, a fresh 17-year low, with the Bank of Korea conducting emergency market stabilization operations. Trading volume hit 15.2 trillion won as foreign investors dumped a net 1.8 trillion won of Korean stocks.

So what: Emergency BOK meeting expected within 48 hours

South Korea's Defense Startups Surge as Global Conflicts Flare

Korean defense technology startups are attracting record investment as global conflicts drive demand for unmanned systems, electronic warfare, and AI-powered military capabilities. Venture funding for Korean defense tech reached $450 million in 2025, triple the previous year, with companies like Hanwha Aerospace and LIG Nex1 acquiring smaller firms. The government's Defense Innovation 4.0 initiative aims to position Korea as a top-five global arms exporter by 2030.

Chinese firms advertise TV and EV exports to North Korea on social media
Guangzhou-based Huaju Electric Appliance Company posted videos on Douyin showing ultra-thin TV exports to North Korea, while other firms advertised electric vehicle sales. The public social media posts suggest apparent violations of international sanctions that ban luxury goods and most electronics exports to the DPRK. The brazen nature of the advertising indicates either lax enforcement or companies betting on continued China-DPRK trade normalization.

South Korea's early exports show resilience despite Iran turmoil
Korean exports in the first 20 days of March maintained growth momentum despite Middle East tensions, with semiconductor shipments continuing their recovery streak into a fifth consecutive month. The data suggests Korea's export engine remains robust even as geopolitical risks mount, though energy import costs are rising sharply with oil approaching $100 per barrel. Export growth to China and Southeast Asia offset weakness in Middle Eastern markets.
Business · SCMP Korea
Korea's 'hurry-hurry' culture keeps killing factory workers
South Korea's industrial accident rate remains among the OECD's highest, with speed-focused work culture contributing to persistent safety failures despite decades of economic development. Recent factory accidents highlight how Korea's rapid industrialization legacy continues to prioritize productivity over worker safety. The government announced new workplace safety measures following a series of high-profile industrial deaths.
Energy · CNA Korea
LG Chem shuts No. 2 ethylene plant at Yeosu complex
LG Chem shut down its second ethylene cracker at the Yeosu petrochemical complex for unplanned maintenance, removing 320,000 tons of annual production capacity from the tight Asian market. The shutdown compounds supply constraints as regional petrochemical demand recovers, with spot ethylene prices in Asia rising 15% this month. Korean petrochemical producers face margin pressure from high oil prices and Middle East supply disruptions.

Kim Jong Un reappointed for third term amid leadership reshuffle
North Korea's parliament reappointed Kim Jong Un for a third term as president of state affairs during Sunday's Supreme People's Assembly session, while elevating key loyalists to senior positions. The leadership reshuffle included promoting close aides to top legislative posts, signaling Kim's continued consolidation of power. Constitutional revision discussions are expected at the assembly's second day session.
