Top Stories

South Korea Names BIS Official as Next Central Bank Chief

President Lee Jae Myung nominated Shin Hyun-song, a senior economist at the Bank for International Settlements, as the next governor of the Bank of Korea on Sunday. Shin, known for his research on global capital flows and financial stability, pledged a 'balanced approach' to monetary policy as Korea faces slowing growth and persistent inflation pressures amid Middle East-driven energy shocks.

So what: Confirmation hearing expected before April monetary policy meeting where markets anticipate rate decision

S. Korea's exports jump 50.4 pct in first 20 days of March

South Korea's exports surged 50.4% year-on-year to $37.8 billion in the first 20 days of March, driven by semiconductor shipments that rose 65.8% and automobile exports up 23.1%. The dramatic increase, building on February's 15.6% gain, signals robust demand recovery despite global economic uncertainties and positions Korea for its strongest quarterly export performance since 2022.

So what: Export strength supports BOK's monetary policy flexibility ahead of April rate decision

Pattern: Largest 20-day export surge since March 2022 commodity boom

Yonhap

North Korean parliament elevates Kim aides, set to tackle constitution revision

North Korea's Supreme People's Assembly opened its 15th session Sunday, formally re-electing Kim Jong Un as state leader and promoting key aides to senior positions. The parliament is expected to revise the constitution to designate South Korea as a 'principal enemy' and remove unification clauses, implementing Kim's January 2024 directive to abandon inter-Korean reconciliation.

So what: Constitutional changes would formalize DPRK's hostile state designation before April 15 holiday

Business · Yonhap
Gov't, ruling party agree upon 25 tln-won supplementary budget amid Middle East crisis
The government and ruling Democratic Party agreed Sunday on a 25 trillion won ($16.6 billion) supplementary budget to address economic impacts from the Middle East crisis, including surging oil prices and supply chain disruptions. The budget, to be submitted for National Assembly approval by April 10, allocates funds for energy subsidies, strategic petroleum reserve expansion, and support for affected industries.
Energy · Yonhap
Finance minister calls for preparation for prolonged Middle East crisis
Finance Minister Koo Yun-cheol warned Sunday that Korea must prepare for a prolonged Middle East crisis that could push oil prices above $120 per barrel and disrupt global supply chains. Koo convened an emergency economic ministers' meeting to coordinate responses including expanding strategic reserves, diversifying energy imports, and preparing targeted support for vulnerable sectors.

Bank of Korea's newly appointed chief pledges 'balanced' policy
Shin Hyun-song, nominated as Bank of Korea governor, pledged to pursue 'balanced' monetary policy that considers both inflation control and financial stability. The BIS economist, known for his work on global capital flows and macroprudential policy, would replace the current governor at a critical juncture as Korea faces slowing growth, persistent inflation, and Middle East-driven energy price shocks.

Korea takes major stride in prosecution reform with passage of bills
The National Assembly passed two landmark bills Friday and Saturday that will separate prosecutorial powers to investigate and indict, creating distinct agencies for each function by October. The reforms, fulfilling a key Lee administration pledge, aim to prevent abuse of prosecutorial power by dividing the current unified system that has drawn criticism for political investigations.

President Lee Jae Myung ordered Sunday that government officials owning multiple properties be excluded from real estate policy decisions, citing potential conflicts of interest.
