Top Stories

Seoul shares crash over 6 pct on escalating U.S.-Iran conflict

South Korean stocks plummeted over 6 percent Monday as escalating US-Iran tensions over the Strait of Hormuz spooked investors, with the KOSPI closing at 5,405.75. The won hit a fresh 17-year low against the dollar at 1,485.93, while oil prices surged on supply disruption fears.

Yonhap

N. Korean leader's sister rejects Japan's summit proposal

Kim Yo Jong rejected Japanese Prime Minister Takaichi's proposal for a summit, stating any meeting is impossible unless Tokyo drops its 'anachronistic hostile policy,' specifically citing the abduction issue. The statement, carried by KCNA, marks North Korea's first official response to Japan's repeated diplomatic overtures since Takaichi took office in November 2025.

Yonhap

Lee stresses self-reliant defense, citing 'very complex' global situation

President Lee Jae Myung emphasized the importance of building self-reliant defense capabilities Monday, describing the current global security environment as 'very complex.' Lee made the remarks during a meeting with senior military officials, as Seoul faces pressure over Middle East tensions and ongoing alliance coordination challenges.

Russian President Vladimir Putin congratulated Kim Jong Un on his reelection as president of state affairs and expressed hope for strengthened bilateral relations. US-Korea · Korea Times
Korea weighs noncombat role in Hormuz tension
Seoul is leaning toward diplomatic and nonmilitary support for freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz rather than sending troops, amid Washington's pressure for allies to help stabilize shipping routes. Korea joined a seven-nation statement condemning Iran's effective closure of the strait, while a senior Democratic Party official indicated the government's position is taking shape gradually.
ROK Policy · Yonhap English
Special counsel raids prosecution offices over ex-first lady case cover-up
The second comprehensive special counsel team raided the Supreme Prosecutors Office and Seoul Central District Prosecutors Office over allegations of intentionally dropping stock manipulation charges against former first lady Kim Keon Hee in October 2024. The raids targeted anti-corruption divisions suspected of abuse of power in the decision not to indict Kim over her alleged involvement in a Deutsch Motors stock price manipulation scheme.
DPRK · AP Korea
North Korea says summit with Japan off unless Tokyo drops 'anachronistic' ways
Kim Yo Jong, sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, rejected Japan's summit proposal in a statement through KCNA, demanding Tokyo abandon its 'anachronistic hostile policy' particularly regarding the abduction issue. The statement marks Pyongyang's first official response to Japanese PM Takaichi's repeated overtures for a leaders' summit to address bilateral issues.
Business · Yonhap English
Samsung Electronics' unionized workers meet co-CEO ahead of proposed walkout
Unionized workers of Samsung Electronics met Monday with co-CEO to discuss wage negotiations ahead of a potential strike action. The meeting comes as the company's first-ever labor union pushes for better compensation amid record semiconductor profits driven by AI chip demand.
Energy · Korea Times
3 dead in wind turbine fire in Yeongdeok county
Three workers died in a wind turbine fire at a power complex in Yeongdeok county, 240 kilometers southeast of Seoul. The blaze began at 1:11 p.m. with two turbine blades catching fire and falling to the ground, while authorities deployed 11 helicopters and 148 personnel to contain the incident.
