Top Stories

Seoul stocks end at fresh record high on revived hope for AI boom

Seoul stocks closed at a fresh peak 6,690.90 on Wednesday, jumping 4.25% on revived hope for AI boom. The rally came as Nvidia executive held meetings with Samsung and SK Hynix executives for physical AI partnerships.

So what: Supports Korea's positioning as global AI hardware leader ahead of expected semiconductor demand surge.

Yonhap

Appeals court raises Yoon's term to 7 years over obstruction of duty charges

Seoul High Court increased former President Yoon Suk Yeol's prison sentence to seven years from five years for obstructing his arrest and tampering with documents over martial law handling. This case is separate from the insurrection case that resulted in life sentence February 19.

So what: Consolidates legal precedent for presidential accountability while Yoon faces additional Marine death probe trial.

Korea Times

Ex-POWs sue North Korea over forced labor

Five former South Korean prisoners of war filed compensation lawsuit against North Korea and Kim Jong-un at Seoul court, seeking 21 million won ($14,200) each. The war survivors aged 95-98 were forced to work in mines after 1953 truce before defecting in early 2000s.

So what: Third such lawsuit following similar cases in 2020 and 2023, establishing legal precedent for wartime reparations claims.
