The Surprising Source of North Korea's Enduring Power

WSJ analysis examines North Korea's resilience through its expanding partnerships with Russia and China, highlighting how arms sales to Russia and renewed diplomatic engagement with Beijing provide economic lifelines. The piece notes that North Korea's strategic value has increased amid great power competition, with both Moscow and Beijing viewing Pyongyang as a useful counterweight to US influence in Northeast Asia.

Wall Street Journal

Rejected by Dozens of Emergency Rooms: South Korea's Medical Crisis

The New York Times reports on South Korea's deepening medical crisis as emergency room rejections surge amid the ongoing doctor strike. Patients are being turned away from dozens of hospitals before finding care, with some dying during transport between facilities. The crisis has exposed structural problems in Korea's healthcare system as over 12,000 resident doctors remain on strike protesting the government's medical school expansion plan.

Exports surge 36.7% in first 10 days of April on semiconductor demand
South Korea's exports jumped 36.7% from a year earlier during the first 10 days of April, driven by robust semiconductor demand. Chip exports likely surged over 50% as AI data center demand accelerates globally. The strong performance reflects recovering global trade and supports the government's 9% annual export growth target for 2026.

Seoul stocks open sharply lower on failed US-Iran peace talks
South Korean stocks opened 2.08% lower Monday, the deepest intraday decline in four months, after US-Iran peace talks collapsed in Islamabad. The Korean won weakened markedly against the dollar as investors fled risk assets amid fears of renewed Middle East conflict and potential oil supply disruptions.
