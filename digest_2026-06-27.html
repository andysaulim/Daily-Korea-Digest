China-Korea

Nearly 10 Chinese and Russian military aircraft enter Korea's KADIZ; ROK scrambles fighter jets

The ROK Joint Chiefs of Staff reported nearly 10 Chinese and Russian military aircraft briefly entered and exited the Korea Air Defense Identification Zone above eastern and southern waters on June 27, with Seoul scrambling Air Force fighter jets. The aircraft did not violate Korean territorial airspace; the JCS assessed the incursion occurred during joint Sino-Russian air drills. KADIZ entries by combined Chinese and Russian aircraft have occurred periodically since 2019.

So what: The incident directly feeds into ongoing ROK-Japan defense coordination discussions as Japanese Defense Minister Koizumi arrived in Seoul on the same day.

US-Korea

Seoul court sentences former ROK First Lady Kim Keon-hee to seven years in prison for bribery

A South Korean court sentenced former first lady Kim Keon-hee to seven years in prison for accepting luxury gifts — described by SCMP as 'high-value precious metals' — tied to favors, in a case that drew international attention from the Washington Post, Al Jazeera, and South China Morning Post. The verdict follows the broader judicial reckoning stemming from former President Yoon Suk Yeol's martial law declaration on December 3, 2024. Former Justice Minister Park Sung-jae, separately sentenced to 25 years for his role in the insurrection, filed an appeal on June 27.

So what: The sentence is a domestic political milestone for President Lee Jae-myung's administration as it consolidates accountability proceedings against Yoon-era officials.

US cybersecurity firm SentinelLabs identified a new malware family called 'macOS.Gaslight,' attributed to North Korean-linked cybercriminals, which combines data-stealing capabilities with a novel mechanism designed to manipulate AI-powered security tools.
