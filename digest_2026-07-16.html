North Korea grants Russian tourists exclusive first access to Ukraine war museum with NATO trophies and DPRK soldier remains

North Korea gave Russian tourists exclusive access to its new Memorial Museum of Combat Feats — the first foreign tourists permitted to tour the facility — which contains the remains of thousands of DPRK soldiers who fought in Ukraine alongside destroyed Western military vehicles and weapons, according to a Swedish tour operator cited by NK News. The museum's framing of DPRK soldiers as combat heroes fighting alongside Russia constitutes the clearest public institutionalization to date of the NK-Russia military alliance narrative. The exclusive access for Russian tourists ahead of any other foreign group underscores the symbolic and political weight Pyongyang attaches to the partnership.

US-Korea · Korea Times
ROK gov't and ruling party agree to integrated Army-Navy-Air Force academy in Daejeon for AI-era OPCON transition
Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back announced at the National Assembly that the government and Democratic Party agreed to establish an integrated military academy at Daejeon's Jaundae compound, designed to train officers in AI-powered all-domain operations in preparation for OPCON transfer. The academy will replace the separate Army, Navy, and Air Force academies, with an academic system emphasizing autonomy, interdisciplinary thinking, and high technological literacy explicitly linked to the post-OPCON combined defense posture with US forces. The OPCON transfer timeline remains subject to ROK-US negotiation, making the academy's design and curriculum a political as well as operational signal.

The analysis provides material evidence that the June 25 plenum directives are being converted into physical production capacity, not merely political statements. DPRK · NK Pro
NK Pro: Second rocket engine test in two months at Sohae; military satellite launch preparations assessed
NK Pro satellite imagery analysis shows what appears to be at least a second rocket engine test at the Sohae Satellite Launch Station within two months, assessed as likely preparation to resume military reconnaissance satellite launches. The Sohae facility previously recorded a 2,500 kN engine test stand firing and has a new viewing area building nearing completion. Two successive engine tests in rapid succession at Sohae, timed against the July 27 armistice anniversary 11 days out, are consistent with DPRK pre-launch preparation cycles.
Japan-Korea · Hankyoreh English
UNESCO finds Japan's depiction of forced Korean labor at Sado mines insufficient
UNESCO determined that Japan's on-site depiction of the forced labor of Korean workers at the Sado Island gold mines is insufficient, reigniting the bilateral history dispute that emerged when Sado was nominated for World Heritage status. Japan had pledged to acknowledge the wartime labor history as part of the 2024 inscription process, and Seoul is expected to press for stronger on-site acknowledgment at Sado. The finding arrives as Japan-Korea diplomatic relations are otherwise in a period of managed engagement under the current administrations.
Energy · Korea Times
Korea breaks ground on $2.5B offshore wind farm — first large-scale project funded entirely by domestic capital
Korea broke ground on the 390-megawatt Shinan UI offshore wind project off South Jeolla Province, a 3 trillion won ($2.5 billion) development funded entirely by domestic capital — the first such project in the country without foreign technology financing or international finance. Climate, Energy, and Environment Minister Kim Sung-hwan called it 'the first large-scale offshore wind project driven entirely by domestic capital,' marking a strategic shift as East Asian renewable energy has historically relied on foreign technology and international finance. The project is part of Korea's broader green transition strategy as it manages energy cost pressures reported separately by the Dong-A Ilbo as rising due to wartime energy dynamics.
