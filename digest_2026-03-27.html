Top Stories

N. Korea, Belarus hold summit, sign friendship treaty

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un held summit talks with Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko and signed a comprehensive strategic partnership treaty during Lukashenko's one-day visit to Pyongyang. The agreement covers political, economic, and security cooperation as Kim expands diplomatic outreach beyond traditional allies Russia and China, potentially positioning Belarus as a mediator for future Trump-Kim talks. Lukashenko's brief visit included ceremonial elements at the Kumsusan Palace of the Sun where Kim Il Sung and Kim Jong Il lie in state, and a state reception featuring cultural performances.

Lee vows to swiftly retake wartime command from U.S., speed up military reform

President Lee Jae-myung declared South Korea will swiftly retake wartime operational control from the United States and accelerate military reforms including selective conscription implementation during a ceremony honoring 55 fallen troops in Yellow Sea incidents. Lee emphasized building peace through strong defense capabilities, marking a significant shift from the previous administration's approach to alliance management. The pledge comes as South Korea faces degraded missile defense coverage after US redeployment of THAAD assets to the Middle East.

Yonhap

Iran threatens Korean vessels doing business with US

Iranian authorities announced Korean-flagged vessels conducting business with the United States will face restrictions when transiting through the Strait of Hormuz, escalating tensions in the vital shipping lane. The threat directly impacts South Korea's energy security as approximately 70% of the country's oil imports transit through the strategic waterway. South Korean military manpower agency confirmed 40 ROK nationals are currently serving onboard ship reserve duty in the Hormuz Strait region.

DPRK · Yonhap English
N. Korea resumes border fortification work after winter hiatus
North Korea has resumed construction efforts to reinforce the northern side of the inter-Korean border after a winter pause, according to South Korea's Joint Chiefs of Staff. The fortification activities include laying mines, erecting anti-tank barriers, and reinforcing tactical roads along the Military Demarcation Line. The resumption follows North Korea's constitutional revision defining South Korea as a hostile state and Kim Jong Un's orders to permanently fortify the border.
US-Korea · Yonhap English
Han Kang's 'We Do Not Part' wins NBCC Award for haunting portrayal of trauma
Nobel laureate Han Kang won the National Book Critics Circle Award for fiction for her novel 'We Do Not Part,' which explores the 1948-49 Jeju Uprising through a contemporary lens. The book, translated by E. Yaewon and Paige Aniyah Morris, beat finalists including Rachel Kushner and Percival Everett. Han's win marks continued international recognition of Korean literature following her 2016 Man Booker Prize and 2024 Nobel Prize in Literature.
Business · Dong-A English
Rising births slow Korea's population decline
South Korea's population decline showed signs of slowing as births increased for the first time in nine years, with preliminary data indicating a potential demographic turning point. The uptick follows the Lee administration's expanded child benefits and housing support programs launched in 2025. Demographers caution that sustained policy support will be needed to reverse the long-term decline trend that has persisted since 2020.
NK-Russia-China · Yonhap English
China's Xi sends congratulatory letter to N. Korea's Kim on reelection
Chinese President Xi Jinping sent a congratulatory message to Kim Jong Un on his reelection as president of state affairs at North Korea's Supreme People's Assembly session. Xi reaffirmed China's commitment to developing bilateral relations and maintaining regional stability through traditional friendship. The message comes as North Korea diversifies its diplomatic engagement beyond China, signing a new strategic partnership with Belarus.
