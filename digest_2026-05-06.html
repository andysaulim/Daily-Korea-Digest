Top Stories

KOSPI surges past 7,380 for 1st time in history on AI boom

South Korea's benchmark KOSPI index shot up 11.63% to close at a record 7,384.56, driven by semiconductor stocks amid global AI optimism and Middle East ceasefire hopes. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix led gains as investors bet on continued AI-driven chip demand. The won strengthened 2.7% against the dollar to 1,447.29, while foreign investors net purchased 1.2 trillion won of Korean stocks.

So what: Directly affects May 22 BOK rate decision calculus

Yonhap

S. Korea to conduct 1st joint firepower exercise under Lee gov't

South Korea will conduct an integrated military firepower exercise in May, the first under the Lee Jae-myung administration. The exercise will demonstrate joint capabilities across army, navy, air force and marine units at the Seungjin Fire Training Field. The drill comes as US-ROK forces recently completed the Korea Marine Exercise Program (KMEP) with US Marines demonstrating aviation ground support capabilities.

So what: Signals continuity in alliance military readiness despite political transition

Yonhap

Judge who convicted Korea's ex-first lady last month found dead

A judge who convicted former first lady Kim Keon-hee last month in a high-profile corruption case was found dead in an apparent suicide. The judge had ruled against the former first lady in a case involving luxury gifts and influence peddling that contributed to the previous administration's downfall. The death comes as special prosecutors are investigating alleged cover-ups by justice officials in related cases.

Iran denies involvement in explosion on S. Korean vessel in Strait of Hormuz
Iran rejected any military role in the blast aboard a South Korean-operated freight vessel in the strategic waterway. Seoul says several days needed to determine the cause of the explosion that damaged the HMM-operated ship, which was being towed to Dubai for repairs. The incident adds pressure on Seoul amid US calls to join maritime security operations near Iran.
DPRK · Yonhap English
N. Korea's revised constitution defines territory, drops unification references
North Korea's updated constitution includes new territorial definitions while removing previous references to Korean Peninsula reunification. The constitutional revision reflects Kim Jong Un's January 2024 declaration that unification is no longer possible and South Korea should be defined as a hostile state. The changes formalize Pyongyang's shift away from decades of unification rhetoric.
Japan-Korea · Yonhap English
Japan PM Takaichi mulls visit to S. Korea's Andong in May for talks with Lee
Japanese Prime Minister Takaichi Sanae is considering a visit to Andong, South Korea in May for a summit with President Lee Jae-myung. The potential meeting would be their first bilateral summit since Lee's inauguration in February 2026. Andong holds significance as a symbol of Korean-Japanese historical ties through the Hahoe Folk Village.
US-Korea · Hankyoreh English
Fire on Korean ship adds to Seoul's dilemma amid US pressure on Hormuz
The explosion on a Korean vessel in the Strait of Hormuz complicates Seoul's response to Washington's renewed push for ROK participation in maritime security operations. Trump administration has repeatedly urged South Korea to join Project Freedom, the US-led naval mission protecting commercial shipping near Iran. Seoul has resisted deployment citing its energy imports from the Middle East.
