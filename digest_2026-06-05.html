Kim Jong Un unveils new nuclear enrichment facility

Kim Jong Un inspected a newly revealed uranium enrichment facility and called for exponentially increasing North Korea's nuclear weapons arsenal, according to state media reports Friday. The leader toured production halls containing centrifuge cascades and declared the need to increase the number of centrifuges to boost weapons-grade material output. This marks the first public disclosure of a uranium enrichment site since the 2010 revelation of the Yongbyon facility, signaling North Korea's expanding nuclear infrastructure.

Seoul stocks plunge 5% on tech selloff, Iran fears

South Korean stocks tumbled more than 5 percent Friday as foreign investors dumped technology shares amid escalating Middle East tensions and concerns over Iran's nuclear program. The KOSPI index fell 410.33 points to close at 8,160.59, its sharpest one-day drop in recent months, with Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix leading declines. The selloff coincided with the won's plunge to a 17-year low, creating a double blow to market sentiment and raising concerns about financial stability.

Election chief resigns over ballot shortage chaos
National Election Commission Chairman Roh Tae-ak offered to resign Friday after 14 Seoul polling stations ran out of ballots during Wednesday's local elections, forcing hundreds of voters to wait hours past the 6 p.m. closing time. The unprecedented failure prompted police investigation and sharp public criticism of Korea's election management system. Prime Minister Kim Min-seok called for parliamentary investigation or independent counsel probe to determine accountability.

South Korea remains committed to building nuclear-powered submarines domestically despite technical challenges and US reluctance to share nuclear propulsion technology.
