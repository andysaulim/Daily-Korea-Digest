Top Stories

Special envoy to Iran met senior Tehran officials over Hormuz blockade

South Korea's special envoy to Iran has met senior officials in Tehran to discuss the U.S. naval blockade of Iranian ports and its impact on Korean energy supplies and shipping routes. The meetings come as Korean companies face severe disruptions to oil imports and container traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, through which 70% of Korea's crude oil imports transit. Seoul is seeking diplomatic channels to ensure safe passage for Korean-flagged vessels and maintain energy security amid the escalating crisis.

So what: Directly affects Korea's energy imports and April oil delivery schedules

South Korean president's Holocaust remarks spark outcry from Israel

President Lee Jae-myung's use of Holocaust imagery to criticize Israel's Gaza operations has triggered a diplomatic crisis, with Israel's Foreign Ministry summoning the Korean ambassador for a formal protest. Lee showed a 1943 Warsaw Ghetto uprising video during a public address, drawing parallels to current Gaza conditions and calling for humanitarian intervention. The controversy has divided Korean public opinion, with critics warning the remarks could damage Korea's Middle East relationships and defense export opportunities.

So what: Complicates Korea's balanced Middle East diplomacy amid defense export ambitions

Reuters

The Surprising Source of North Korea's Enduring Power

North Korea's resilience stems from its unique fusion of traditional Korean Confucian hierarchy with Stalinist state control, creating a system more durable than other communist regimes, according to new analysis. The Kim dynasty's appropriation of neo-Confucian legitimacy concepts, combined with juche ideology's emphasis on hereditary succession, has created deeper societal roots than purely ideological communist states. This hybrid model explains why North Korea survived the Soviet collapse while maintaining stricter social control than China or Vietnam achieved through market reforms.

So what: Challenges assumptions about regime change scenarios in contingency planning

S. Korea closely monitoring developments after U.S. announces blockade of Iranian ports
South Korea is closely monitoring the latest developments in the Middle East after the United States announced a blockade of all maritime traffic to and from Iranian ports following failed peace talks, Cheong Wa Dae said Monday. Solo in Seoul? South Korean capital offers helpers for US$4 an hour
Seoul Metropolitan Government announced a new service offering personal companions for solo travelers and residents at 5,500 won ($4) per hour. The program aims to combat loneliness in the capital where single-person households now comprise over 30% of all households. Companions can accompany clients to restaurants, shopping districts, cultural events, or simply provide conversation, addressing Korea's growing social isolation challenge.

South Korea and Poland to upgrade ties as Tusk calls Seoul key ally after US
Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk and South Korean leaders agreed Monday to elevate bilateral relations to a comprehensive strategic partnership during Tusk's Seoul visit. Poland described South Korea as its most important ally after the United States, highlighting defense cooperation including Warsaw's purchase of K2 tanks, K9 howitzers, and FA-50 fighter jets worth over $12 billion. The upgraded partnership includes expanded defense industrial cooperation, with Poland seeking Korean investment in modernizing its defense production capabilities.
Business · Korea Times
Coinone fined 5.2 billion won, faces biz suspension over lapses
The Financial Intelligence Unit fined Coinone, one of Korea's major crypto exchanges, 5.2 billion won ($3.49 million) and imposed a three-month partial business suspension over anti-money laundering failures. The exchange failed to verify identities in about 70,000 cases and supported some 10,000 transactions with 16 unregistered overseas exchanges. During the suspension, new customers cannot deposit or withdraw money for crypto trading, potentially affecting Korea's crypto market structure.
Trilateral · Yonhap English
Assembly speaker, Polish PM discuss expansion of partnership
National Assembly Speaker Woo Won-shik met with Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk on Monday to discuss expanding bilateral cooperation beyond defense to include energy, infrastructure, and nuclear power. The meeting comes as Poland positions itself as a key European hub for Korean companies amid growing trade tensions and supply chain realignments. Both sides emphasized shared democratic values and the potential for Poland to serve as a gateway for Korean businesses entering the European market.
Business · South China Morning Post
Foreigners with 'close ties' to South Koreans will receive cash aid
The Korean government announced that foreign residents with close family ties to Korean nationals will be eligible for the supplementary budget cash relief payments. This marks the first time non-citizens can receive direct cash support, covering spouses and immediate family members of Korean citizens. The policy shift reflects Korea's evolving demographics, with foreign residents now comprising over 2.5 million people, and aims to address criticism that previous relief measures excluded mixed-nationality households despite their tax contributions.
