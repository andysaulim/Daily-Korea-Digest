Top Stories

Kim Jong Un and Lukashenko sign friendship treaty in Pyongyang

Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko and North Korean leader Kim Jong Un signed a friendship and cooperation deal Thursday covering agriculture, education, health care, business, science and information exchanges. Lukashenko arrived Wednesday for his first visit to North Korea, welcomed with ceremony at Pyongyang airport. The agreement deepens ties between two Russian-aligned states as Moscow cultivates anti-Western coalition.

Pattern: First Belarus-DPRK summit follows pattern of Russia expanding partner network with isolated states

South Korea to Buy Back $3.3 Billion of Bonds to Curb Volatility

South Korea announced plans to repurchase $3.3 billion worth of government bonds as part of broader market stabilization measures amid Middle East tensions. The buyback targets long-term bonds to reduce volatility after yields spiked on Iran conflict concerns. Finance Ministry also unveiled $17 billion emergency budget package described as 'wartime' economic mobilization to address energy security and inflation pressures.

Bloomberg

Korea raises oil price cap as Middle East conflict drives energy crisis

Government will raise oil price ceilings effective Friday following cross-ministerial meeting at Cheong Wa Dae to assess Iran conflict impact on Korea's economy. Prices per liter will rise to 1,934 won for gasoline, 1,923 won for diesel, and 1,530 won for kerosene in second biweekly update since March 13. Decision balances international price increases against citizens' daily living costs while implementing series of energy security measures.
