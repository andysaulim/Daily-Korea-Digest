US-Korea · Yonhap English
Lee pledges utmost efforts to avoid repeat of 2014 Sewol ferry disaster
President Lee Jae-myung marked the 12th anniversary of the Sewol ferry tragedy by pledging government reforms to prevent future disasters and ensure maritime safety. Memorial services were held nationwide for the 304 victims, mostly high school students, who died when the overloaded ferry capsized off Jindo island. Lee stated he feels 'heavy responsibility' and promised comprehensive safety system overhauls.
Technology · Dong-A English
Study links Korean crater to oxygen rise
International research team identified Jeju volcanic crater's role in Earth's atmospheric oxygen increase 850 million years ago. The finding connects Korean geological features to global climate history and early life evolution. Research published in Nature Geoscience demonstrates how regional volcanic activity influenced planetary-scale atmospheric changes.
Energy · Diplomat
South Korea takes harder line on Israel amid energy security concerns
Seoul's evolving Middle East policy reflects growing independence from US positions as energy supply disruptions threaten economic stability. Korean refineries secure alternative crude supplies from Latin America while diplomatic messaging shifts toward regional stability over alliance solidarity. The recalibration signals Lee administration's willingness to pursue autonomous foreign policy when core economic interests are at stake.

Seoul stresses market economy in formal response to USTR trade probe
South Korean government submitted formal opinion to USTR's Section 301 investigation emphasizing commitment to market economy principles and forced labor eradication efforts. The submission addresses US concerns about state subsidies while defending Korea's WTO-compliant trade practices. Response comes as Section 301 probe threatens new tariff authority after July's Section 122 expiry.
