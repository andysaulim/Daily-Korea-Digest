Top Stories

Seoul sends technical team to Dubai to investigate Hormuz ship attack

South Korea sent a technical analysis team to Dubai to investigate the May 4 attack on the South Korean vessel HMM Namu in the Strait of Hormuz. A senior Seoul official said there is low possibility that an actor other than Iran was involved in the attack. Foreign Minister Cho Hyun announced additional investigations and diplomatic responses are planned.

So what: Directly affects Seoul's decision on joining US-led Hormuz security operations after Trump urged Korean participation.

KOSPI hits record 7,981.41, approaches historic 8,000 milestone

South Korean stocks finished at a record peak of 7,981.41, surging 6.56% as investors responded positively to the ongoing US-China summit in Beijing. The benchmark index came within striking distance of the psychologically important 8,000 level for the first time in history. Market optimism centered on potential diplomatic breakthroughs reducing regional tensions.

So what: The 8,000 level represents a key psychological barrier; breakthrough could trigger additional foreign investment inflows.

Yonhap English

US, North Korea appear unprepared for summit but possibility cannot be ruled out

Preparations for a summit between US President Donald Trump and North Korean leader Kim Jong Un appear incomplete according to a Seoul official, though the possibility cannot be completely ruled out. The assessment comes amid the ongoing Trump-Xi summit in Beijing which could create diplomatic openings. South Korea is monitoring whether the US-China talks might lead to renewed US-DPRK engagement.

NKPro
North Korea has completed dismantlement of ROK-built facilities signaling end to previous inter-Korean cooperation frameworks.
NK Pro analysis of satellite imagery confirms North Korea finished dismantling the South Korean liaison office and family reunion hotel in Kaesong. The facilities represented millions in ROK investment during previous détente periods. Dismantlement began in December 2024 and marks definitive end to infrastructure from earlier cooperation.
So what: Affects Moon administration legacy evaluation and prospects for renewed inter-Korean dialogue infrastructure.
