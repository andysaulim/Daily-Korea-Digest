Risk
Dark emails are the hardest to render reliably — Outlook and Gmail both meddle with dark backgrounds. Heavy over 15 sections; best borrowed as an accent, not the whole product.

Recommendation

Direction B (Briefing book), with C's monospace numerals in the data displays. Your product's entire framing — CSIS Korea Chair, PDB-style, expert audience — is already the briefing-book story; the visual identity should match the editorial identity. The cream ground is also the most robust choice technically: it degrades gracefully under Gmail's dark-mode color meddling, where both pure white and near-black get force-inverted unpredictably.

Direction A is the safe fallback if you want zero reader disruption. I'd take C's tabular monospace numerals into the market strip, polling dashboard, and facility grid regardless of direction — that's a pure win for scannability with no rendering risk.
