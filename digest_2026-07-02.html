Business

KOSPI plunges nearly 8% on tech selloff amid renewed AI concerns; KRX activates sidecar

Seoul stocks plunged nearly 8 percent Thursday as a global tech selloff driven by renewed AI concerns battered semiconductor and technology shares; the Korea Exchange activated its sell-side sidecar circuit breaker as the decline accelerated. Foreign investors dumped local equities, pushing the won weaker against the dollar, even as Federal Reserve Governor Warsh's remarks on inflation prompted a brief late-session partial recovery. The OECD separately warned Thursday of South Korea's rising vulnerability to external shocks from its heavy dependence on semiconductor exports.

So what: The KOSPI rout and OECD semiconductor concentration warning directly bear on the finance ministry's first US investment project review meeting held the same day and on the BOK's rate calculus ahead of the July 10 Monetary Policy Board meeting.

Technology

Samsung, SK Hynix to build HBM packaging fabs in Chungcheong as part of 392 tln won investment

Samsung and SK Hynix will construct high-bandwidth memory packaging fabrication facilities in South Korea's Chungcheong region as part of an industrywide 392 trillion-won ($252.5 billion) investment package announced Thursday. President Lee Jae-myung simultaneously pledged to develop the region into the country's center for AI-led innovation. The Chungcheong semiconductor cluster is a second domestic hub intended to diversify from the existing concentration in Gyeonggi Province.

So what: The 392 trillion-won domestic investment package runs alongside the US-bound $350B pledge framework; the finance minister's first US investment review meeting, held the same day, will track the parallel outbound commitment.

Hanwha Ocean selected as preferred bidder for 7.8 tln-won ROK Navy destroyer program

Hanwha Ocean has been selected as the preferred bidder for a 7.8 trillion-won ROK Navy destroyer construction project, the company announced Thursday. The contract represents a major domestic defense-shipbuilding award for Hanwha Ocean, which is simultaneously expanding its US shipyard footprint through its $5 billion Philly Shipyard commitment under the MASGA framework. The destroyer win compounds Hanwha Ocean's position as the ROK's leading naval shipbuilder at a moment when Kim Jong Un has ordered two warships per year built over the next five years.

So what: Directly relevant to both the ROK Navy's force modernization program and to Hanwha Ocean's dual domestic-US shipbuilding posture under the $150B MASGA shipbuilding bucket of the $350B investment pledge.
