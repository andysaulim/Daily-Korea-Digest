Top Stories

Kim Yo Jong rejects Japan's summit proposal with North Korea

Kim Jong Un's powerful sister Kim Yo Jong rejected Japan's proposal for a summit meeting, maintaining Pyongyang's hardline stance against dialogue with Tokyo. In a statement carried by KCNA, Kim dismissed any possibility of talks until Japan addresses historical issues and ends what North Korea calls hostile policies. The rejection comes despite Japan's recent efforts to engage North Korea on the abduction issue and regional security concerns.

Yonhap

Putin congratulates Kim Jong Un on reelection, seeks closer ties

Russian President Vladimir Putin sent a congratulatory message to Kim Jong Un on his reelection as chairman of North Korea's State Affairs Commission, voicing hope for strengthened bilateral cooperation. The message underscores Moscow's continued engagement with Pyongyang amid their deepening military-technical cooperation and weapons transfers. Putin's outreach follows the pattern of high-level exchanges between Russia and North Korea since their September 2023 summit.

Yonhap

Korean won hits 17-year low at 1,492.58 as Iran crisis deepens

The South Korean won plunged to a fresh 17-year low against the US dollar Monday, trading at 1,492.58 as investors dumped emerging market currencies amid escalating tensions between the United States and Iran. The sharp depreciation marks the weakest level for the won since the 2008 global financial crisis, prompting the Bank of Korea to signal potential market stabilization measures. Foreign investors net sold $2.3 billion worth of Korean stocks and bonds in the morning session alone.

Seoul stocks crash over 6% on escalating US-Iran conflict fears
South Korean stocks plummeted over 6 percent Monday as investors fled risk assets amid escalating tensions between the United States and Iran in the Middle East. The benchmark KOSPI index fell 6.2 percent to 5,405.75, marking its steepest single-day decline since March 2020 during the early COVID-19 pandemic. Financial authorities held an emergency meeting to assess market stability and prepare response measures.

North Korea brings loyal cadres to center of power in major reshuffle
North Korea has announced a large-scale personnel reshuffle following Kim Jong Un's reelection, elevating loyalists who have proven their dedication to the regime. The moves appear designed to further consolidate Kim's control as he enters his third term as chairman of the State Affairs Commission. Several military officials involved in recent weapons development programs received prominent positions in the new lineup.
DPRK · Yonhap English
25% of N.Korean defectors from Punggye-ri area show chromosome mutations
A quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near the Punggye-ri nuclear test site show chromosome mutations, according to new medical data. The findings suggest significant radiation exposure among residents living near the test facility where North Korea conducted six nuclear tests between 2006 and 2017. Health authorities plan expanded screening programs for defectors from the northeastern region.

President Lee stresses self-reliant defense amid 'complex' global situation
President Lee Jae Myung on Monday underscored the importance of building self-reliant defense capabilities, citing the "very complex" global security environment. Speaking at a defense industry event, Lee emphasized that South Korea must strengthen its own defense industrial base while maintaining strong alliance cooperation. The remarks come as Seoul navigates tensions between its traditional security partnerships and growing emphasis on strategic autonomy.

PM Kim cancels China trip amid Middle East conflict escalation
Prime Minister Kim Min-seok has scrapped his plans to visit China this week as the government focuses on managing economic fallout from the escalating Middle East crisis. The cancellation reflects Seoul's priority on stabilizing financial markets and ensuring energy security as oil prices surge above $100 per barrel. Kim was scheduled to meet Chinese Premier Li Qiang for talks on bilateral economic cooperation.

The second comprehensive special counsel team on Monday raided senior prosecution offices as part of its investigation into alleged cover-up of cases involving former first lady Kim Keon-hee.
