Top Stories

Seoul shares crash over 6% on escalating U.S.-Iran conflict

South Korean stocks plummeted 6.2% Monday marking the worst single-day drop since March 2020 as the Strait of Hormuz crisis threatened oil supplies, with the KOSPI closing at 5,405.75. The won hit a fresh 17-year low of 1,493.18 per dollar as investors fled to safe havens amid fears of supply chain disruptions, while the exchange issued a sell-side sidecar to curb panic selling. Foreign investors dumped a net 1.2 trillion won worth of Korean shares, the largest outflow in two years.

Yonhap

Putin congratulates Kim Jong Un on reelection, voices hope for closer ties

Russian President Vladimir Putin congratulated Kim Jong Un on his reelection as State Affairs Commission chairman, proposing to expand bilateral cooperation across military and economic sectors in a personal message delivered through diplomatic channels. The message specifically mentioned deepening the comprehensive strategic partnership signed in June 2024, with Putin expressing readiness to implement all provisions including mutual defense commitments. Moscow's outreach follows reports of North Korean troops suffering heavy casualties in Ukraine's Kursk region while fighting alongside Russian forces.

So what: Sets stage for potential Kim-Putin summit ahead of Victory Day celebrations May 9

Yonhap

Lee stresses self-reliant defense, citing 'very complex' global situation

President Lee Jae Myung on Monday underscored the importance of self-reliant defense capabilities during a national security meeting, citing the 'very complex' global security environment with conflicts in Ukraine and the Middle East. Lee directed the military to accelerate indigenous weapons development programs including the KF-21 fighter jet and long-range missile systems while maintaining ironclad readiness against North Korean provocations. The remarks signal continuity in defense modernization despite Lee's Democratic Party's traditional emphasis on inter-Korean engagement.

Pattern: Echoes Park Chung-hee's 1970s self-reliant defense doctrine amid US withdrawal fears

Yonhap

N. Korean leader's sister rejects Japan's summit proposal

Kim Yo Jong, the powerful sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, rejected Japan's latest proposal for a bilateral summit, stating through KCNA that any dialogue must address historical issues including compensation for colonial rule and the abduction issue. She dismissed PM Takaichi's overture as lacking substance and accused Tokyo of using summit proposals for domestic political purposes ahead of July's upper house elections. The statement marks Pyongyang's first official response to Japan's diplomatic outreach under the new Takaichi administration.

N. Korea's Kim brings loyal cadres to center of power to reinforce rule
North Korea announced a large-scale personnel reshuffle following the Supreme People's Assembly session, promoting younger loyalists and military commanders who served in Ukraine to key state positions. The shake-up includes elevating officials from the munitions industry and strategic rocket forces, reflecting Kim Jong Un's focus on weapons modernization and the Russia partnership. Business · Yonhap English
BOK chief nominee Shin steps down from BIS post
Bank of Korea governor nominee Shin Hyun-song resigned from his position at the Bank for International Settlements ahead of confirmation hearings scheduled for next week. Shin, who served as economic adviser at BIS since 2022, is expected to face questions about monetary policy direction as Korea grapples with currency weakness and inflation pressures from the Middle East crisis. His appointment comes at a critical juncture with the won at 17-year lows and markets demanding aggressive intervention.
DPRK · Yonhap English
25% of N. Korean defectors from Punggye-ri area show chromosome mutations
A quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near the Punggye-ri nuclear test site exhibited chromosome abnormalities, according to new medical data released by the Unification Ministry. The findings, covering defectors who lived within 40 kilometers of the test site, raise concerns about radiation exposure from the six nuclear tests conducted there between 2006 and 2017. Similar chromosome damage patterns were found in residents near other nuclear test sites globally.

Trade Minister Yeo Han-koo will attend the World Trade Organization ministerial conference in Abu Dhabi starting March 28 to discuss global trade rules and dispute settlement reform. Yeo plans bilateral meetings with counterparts from Japan, EU, and ASEAN to coordinate responses to US Section 301 investigations.
