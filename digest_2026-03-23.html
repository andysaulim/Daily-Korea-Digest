Top Stories

N. Korea's Kim reappointed as president of state affairs

Kim Jong Un was reappointed as president of state affairs at the first session of North Korea's newly elected 15th Supreme People's Assembly on Monday. The rubber-stamp parliament convened following elections in November 2025, formalizing Kim's continued grip on power. This marks Kim's third five-year term as head of state since the position was created in 2016.

So what: Completes North Korea's post-Party Congress political consolidation ahead of Apr 15 Day of the Sun celebrations

Pattern: Previous SPA reappointments in Apr 2019 and Apr 2014 preceded major diplomatic initiatives within 60 days

Source: Yonhap English

S. Korea's exports jump 50.4 pct in first 20 days of March on robust chip demand

South Korean exports surged 50.4% year-on-year in the first 20 days of March, driven by booming semiconductor demand from global AI infrastructure buildouts. The surge marks the strongest export growth in over a year as memory chip prices recover and AI accelerators drive unprecedented demand.

So what: Export surge supports BOK's hawkish stance and reduces pressure for rate cuts despite global uncertainties

Source: Yonhap English

Lee taps BIS official Shin Hyun-song as BOK chief

President Lee Jae Myung nominated Shin Hyun-song, currently economic adviser at the Bank for International Settlements, as the next Bank of Korea governor on Sunday. Shin brings deep expertise in international finance and macroeconomics, having served at the BIS since 2014. He immediately resigned his BIS post to prepare for National Assembly confirmation hearings.

So what: Shin's international experience positions BOK to navigate complex cross-border capital flows as US-Korea rates diverge

Source: Yonhap English

Business · Yonhap English
Gov't, ruling party agree on 25 tln-won supplementary budget amid Middle East crisis
The government and Democratic Party agreed Sunday on a 25 trillion won supplementary budget to counter economic impacts from the Middle East crisis. The budget focuses on energy security measures and support for affected industries as oil prices surge past $107 per barrel. Finance Minister Koo Yun-cheol called for preparation for a prolonged crisis, with the supplementary budget expected to pass the National Assembly on April 10.
DPRK · Yonhap English
25 pct of N. Korean defectors from Punggye-ri area show chromosome mutations
A quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near the Punggye-ri nuclear test site display chromosome mutations, according to new medical data. The findings suggest radiation exposure from the six nuclear tests conducted at the site between 2006 and 2017. This marks the first systematic documentation of genetic damage in the civilian population living near North Korea's primary nuclear testing facility.

Policy · Korea Times
Korea takes major stride in prosecution reform with passage of bills
The National Assembly passed two landmark bills Friday and Saturday that separate prosecution powers to indict from powers to investigate, creating new specialized agencies. The reforms will dismantle the current prosecutorial office in October, marking the most significant restructuring of Korea's justice system in decades. The bills fulfilled a key campaign promise of President Lee Jae Myung to curtail prosecutorial overreach.
Technology · Maeil Business
Major US robot companies prioritizing transition from Chinese to Korean parts
Leading American robotics manufacturers are accelerating shifts from Chinese suppliers to Korean component makers amid supply chain security concerns. The transition reflects both geopolitical tensions and Korea's advancing capabilities in precision manufacturing and semiconductors. Korean suppliers stand to capture significant market share as US firms implement friend-shoring strategies ahead of potential further trade restrictions.
Business · Yonhap English
Seoul stocks open sharply lower amid renewed energy price woes
The KOSPI plunged 5.67% to 5,436.62 in morning trading Monday as Middle East tensions drove energy prices higher and sparked risk-off sentiment. The Korea Exchange issued a sell-side sidecar as the index breached circuit breaker levels. The won weakened 1.23% to 1,508.13 per dollar as foreign investors fled emerging market assets amid the regional crisis.
