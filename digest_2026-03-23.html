Top Stories

Putin congratulates Kim on reelection, voices hope for closer ties

Russian President Vladimir Putin sent a congratulatory message to Kim Jong Un on his reelection as North Korea's state affairs commission president, expressing hopes to strengthen bilateral ties. Putin emphasized expanding cooperation across political, economic and military spheres, signaling Moscow's commitment to deepening the strategic partnership established during their June 2024 summit.

Yonhap

Seoul shares crash over 6 pct on escalating U.S.-Iran conflict

The KOSPI index plummeted 6.2% to 5,405.75, its sharpest single-day drop since March 2022, as the US-Iran military confrontation in the Strait of Hormuz triggered a regional risk-off. Foreign investors dumped 1.2 trillion won in Korean equities while the won hit a 17-year low of 1,481.28 per dollar. The Korea Exchange activated sell-side sidecar circuit breakers as program trading accelerated the decline.

Pattern: The 6.2% drop matches the scale of the March 2022 Russia-Ukraine invasion selloff.

N. Korean leader's sister rejects Japan's summit proposal

Kim Yo Jong, sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, publicly rejected Japan's proposal for a bilateral summit, marking Pyongyang's first official response to Prime Minister Takaichi's overtures. The statement signals North Korea's unwillingness to engage with Tokyo despite Japan's recent diplomatic initiatives.

25 pct of N. Korean defectors from Punggye-ri region show chromosome mutations
A quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near the Punggye-ri nuclear test site display chromosomal abnormalities consistent with radiation exposure, according to new medical data. The findings from health screenings of 89 defectors suggest widespread contamination affecting civilians living within 40 kilometers of the test facility.
