N. Korean leader's sister rejects Japan's summit proposal

Kim Yo Jong dismissed Japanese Prime Minister Kishida's latest proposal for a leaders' summit, marking the third rejection of Japan's diplomatic outreach since 2024. She cited unresolved issues including Japan's "hostile policies" and historical disputes as barriers to dialogue.

So what: Closes window for Kishida's signature foreign policy initiative before his September 2026 LDP leadership election

Pattern: Third rejection follows pattern from March 2024 and September 2025 rebuffs

Russian President Vladimir Putin sent a congratulatory message to Kim Jong Un on his reelection as State Affairs President, expressing hope for strengthened bilateral cooperation. The message comes as military-technical cooperation between Moscow and Pyongyang continues to expand despite international sanctions.

So what: Signals continued Russia-DPRK axis deepening ahead of potential Kim visit to Russia

Korean won hits 17-yr low as Iran crisis escalates

The won plunged to 1,515.78 per dollar Monday, its weakest level since March 2009, as escalating US-Iran tensions triggered capital flight from emerging markets. The Bank of Korea indicated it may intervene to stabilize currency markets if volatility persists.

So what: Forces BOK to balance currency defense with growth support ahead of March 27 rate decision

Seoul shares crash over 6 pct on US-Iran conflict fears
The KOSPI plummeted 6.2% to 5,405.75 points Monday, triggering a sell-side sidecar as investors fled risk assets amid escalating tensions between the US and Iran. Foreign investors net sold 1.2 trillion won worth of shares, while the won hit a fresh 17-year low of 1,515.78 per dollar.
DPRK · Yonhap English
25 pct of defectors from Punggye-ri show chromosome damage
A quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near the Punggye-ri nuclear test site show chromosome mutations consistent with radiation exposure, according to South Korean medical data. The findings from 114 defectors examined between 2017-2024 provide the first systematic evidence of health impacts from the six nuclear tests conducted at the site.
