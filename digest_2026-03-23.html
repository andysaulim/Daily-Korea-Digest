Top Stories

Seoul stocks crash over 6% as Iran crisis escalates

South Korean stocks plummeted 6.2% Monday as escalating US-Iran conflict over Strait of Hormuz shipping lanes triggered massive sell-offs across Asian markets. KOSPI fell to 5,405.75 while Korean won hit 17-year low at 1,485.46 per dollar amid flight to safe-haven assets. Energy and shipping stocks led declines as oil prices surged past $99 per barrel on supply disruption fears.

Yonhap English

Putin congratulates Kim Jong Un on reelection, voices hope for closer ties

Russian President Vladimir Putin congratulated Kim Jong Un on his reelection as North Korea's State Affairs Commission president, expressing hopes for expanded bilateral cooperation. Congratulatory message delivered through diplomatic channels emphasized strategic partnership between Moscow and Pyongyang amid ongoing Ukraine conflict. Putin's outreach signals continued alignment between the two nations despite international sanctions.

Korea Times

FM Cho urges safe navigation in Strait of Hormuz amid Iran tensions

Foreign Minister Cho Hyun called his Iranian counterpart Monday requesting guarantees for safe passage of Korean-flagged vessels through Strait of Hormuz as US-Iran crisis intensifies. Seoul operates one of world's largest merchant fleets with heavy reliance on Middle East energy imports via the strategic waterway. Call reflects growing concern over potential disruption to Korea's $180 billion annual energy trade.

Financial Times

PM Kim cancels China trip amid Middle East conflict

Prime Minister Kim Min-seok scrapped planned visit to China citing need to monitor escalating Middle East situation and its impact on Korean interests. Cancellation disrupts Seoul's diplomatic schedule as Lee administration seeks to balance relations with Beijing amid US-China tensions. Decision reflects priority given to energy security concerns over routine diplomatic engagement.

DPRK · Reuters
25% of North Korean defectors from Punggye-ri show chromosome mutations
Quarter of North Korean defectors from regions near Punggye-ri nuclear test site exhibit chromosome mutations, according to medical data released by Seoul-based research institute. Findings suggest significant radiation exposure from six underground nuclear tests conducted between 2006-2017. Data provides rare insight into health impacts of DPRK nuclear program on civilian population.

Raids come amid public anger over high fuel costs despite falling crude oil prices.
