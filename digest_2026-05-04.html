Top Stories

Martial law preparations traced to early 2024 by special counsel

Special counsel team discovered Defense Counterintelligence Command showed signs of martial law preparations since first half of 2024, months before Yoon Suk Yeol's December declaration. Investigation of military officials confirmed preparation activities, backing Lee administration's position that the December 3 martial law attempt was long-planned rather than spontaneous. Finding strengthens prosecution's insurrection case against former president and military officials.

So what: Affects ongoing trials of former President Yoon and military commanders charged with insurrection

Special counsel investigation

Lee urges deliberation on special probe into 'fabricated' charges

President Lee Jae Myung stressed need for public input on Democratic Party's bill mandating special counsel probe into alleged fabricated prosecutions during Yoon administration. Bill targets 12 cases including Daejang-dong development scandal where Lee was indicted, with provision allowing withdrawal of ongoing indictments. Hong Ihk-pyo, senior presidential secretary, cited president's call for careful deliberation amid debate over probe's scope.

So what: Bill introduction follows pattern of using special counsels to investigate previous administrations

Presidential Office statement

KOSPI surges 7.12% toward 7,000 on semiconductor rally

Seoul shares posted biggest single-day gain in months, jumping 7.12% to 6,936.99 as chip stocks led rally across Asian markets. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix drove gains while won strengthened to 1,468.78 per dollar amid improved risk sentiment. Foreign investors turned net buyers after recent selling streak, with semiconductor sector recovery expectations fueling optimism.

So what: Market rally coincides with BOK deputy governor's signal on potential rate hike at May 22 meeting

Lee's approval rating drops to 59.5% on cost of living concerns
President Lee Jae-myung's approval fell 2.7 percentage points to 59.5% according to Realmeter poll of 2,006 respondents, marking first drop below 60% in two months. Negative assessments rose to 35%, with decline marked by reduced support among centrist voters and groups sensitive to rising consumer prices amid global inflation pressures.

Seoul High Court will broadcast live the appellate ruling for former Prime Minister Han Duck-soo at 10 a.m. Thursday over his role in Yoon's martial law declaration. Foreign workers in Korea surpass 1.1 million milestone
Employed foreign residents reached 1,109,000 as of May 2025, up 99,000 year-on-year, with student visa holders jumping 71.8% to 56,000 according to Korea Employment Information Service.
