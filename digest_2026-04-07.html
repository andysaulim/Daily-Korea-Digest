Top Stories

Kim Yo-jong says N. Korean leader calls Lee 'frank, broad-minded' for his regret over drone incident

Kim Yo-jong, the powerful sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong-un, said her brother called South Korean President Lee Jae-myung 'frank, broad-minded' for expressing regret over civilian drone incursions into North Korean territory. The statement represents North Korea's swift and unusually positive response to Lee's diplomatic gesture. Kim Yo-jong's public praise suggests Pyongyang may be open to de-escalation after recent tensions over the drone flights.

Yonhap · Apr 6

Samsung Electronics posts record operating profit in Q1, beats expectations

Samsung Electronics estimated its first-quarter operating profit exceeded 50 trillion won, setting a new quarterly record and beating market expectations. The surge was driven by strong demand for AI chips and memory semiconductors amid the artificial intelligence boom. Revenue for the quarter also reached record levels as Samsung capitalized on the global semiconductor shortage and rising chip prices.

Yonhap · Apr 7

Middle East crisis spurs economic risks, inflationary pressure: KDI

South Korea's state-run Korea Development Institute warned that downside risks to the economy are increasing due to the Middle East crisis, citing mounting inflationary pressures from rising oil prices. The KDI assessment highlighted energy costs as the primary transmission channel for regional instability to impact Korean growth. The institute called for proactive fiscal and monetary policy responses to mitigate external shocks.
