DPRK

North Korea tests ability to strike ROK power stations with tactical nukes, per KCNA

KCNA reported that Kim Jong Un oversaw weapons tests specifically designed to demonstrate the DPRK's ability to strike South Korean power stations using tactical nuclear warheads, conducted on the 76th anniversary of the Korean War. The tests followed Kim's June 25 Workers' Party plenum directive to 'exercise its position as a nuclear state' and his June 25 commissioning of the Choe Hyon destroyer. The CSIS provocations database records a MIRV capability test announced exactly two years earlier on June 26, 2024.

So what: Directly affects the agenda for the June 27-28 ROK-Japan defense ministerial talks in Seoul.

Pattern: On June 26, 2024, North Korea announced its first MIRV capability test; the June 26, 2026 tactical nuclear power-grid strike test marks a two-year pattern of Korean War anniversary provocations escalating in declared capability.
