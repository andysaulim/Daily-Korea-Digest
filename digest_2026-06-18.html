DPRK

Kim Yo Jong slams G7 denuclearization call, declares nuclear arms 'core interests' and 'line of no retreat'

Kim Jong Un's sister Kim Yo Jong issued a statement via KCNA on June 18 calling the DPRK's nuclear arsenal 'core interests' and declaring denuclearization a 'line of no retreat,' directly rejecting the G7's call for denuclearization. The statement reinforces the DPRK's hardline posture declared in recent days — 'denuclearization is a matter terminated irreversibly' — and comes on the second anniversary of Putin's June 18, 2024 Pyongyang visit that produced the Treaty on Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Kim Yo Jong's statement framed nuclear weapons as a 'means of self-defense,' echoing the doctrinal language embedded in the September 2022 nuclear force law.

So what: Directly affects the framing of any US-DPRK diplomatic outreach that Trump has signaled interest in, and arrives the day before the NK-Russia Tumen bridge scheduled opening.

Pattern: The CSIS NK provocations database records a DPRK artillery rocket launch on June 19, 2025 and the Putin-Kim Pyongyang summit and treaty signing on June 18-19, 2024 — making mid-June a recurrently active period for DPRK signaling.

Business

KOSPI closes above 9,000 for first time on record, driven by AI chip rally and US-Iran deal

Seoul's KOSPI benchmark closed at 9,063.84 on June 18, surging more than 2 percent past the 9,000 milestone for the first time in the index's history, propelled by AI-driven demand for memory chips and the prospect of lower energy prices following the US-Iran deal. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix were among the leading gainers as HBM and advanced DRAM demand accelerated. The KOSPI has gained 16.74% on a percentage-change basis as of June 18.

So what: Sustained KOSPI strength at these levels tests the BOK's calculus ahead of its next Monetary Policy Board meeting, with 매일경제 reporting growing market expectations of a rate hike.
