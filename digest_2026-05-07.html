Top Stories

North Korea enshrines 'two states' doctrine in constitution

North Korea revised its constitution to formally enshrine the 'two states' doctrine, removing all references to reunification with South Korea. The constitutional changes mark Kim Jong Un's definitive abandonment of unification goals, declaring South Korea a separate hostile state rather than part of a divided nation.

So what: Seoul said it will comprehensively review the constitutional changes but maintain its peaceful coexistence policy

Hankyoreh English

After 1,600 test flights, KF-21 is officially combat-ready

The Korean-made KF-21 Boramae fighter jet received final combat suitability approval from DAPA after completing more than 1,600 test flights over three years. The KF-21 Block-I air-to-air combat variant satisfied all performance requirements, marking completion of the indigenous fighter program after more than a decade of development.

So what: DAPA submits evaluation results to Ministry of National Defense for final ministerial approval

Korea Times

Appeals court cuts ex-PM's sentence to 15 years, but upholds insurrection conviction

Seoul High Court reduced former Prime Minister Han Duck-soo's sentence from 23 to 15 years but upheld his insurrection conviction for role in Yoon Suk Yeol's December 2024 martial law declaration. The court ruled the martial law decree was unconstitutional and that Han helped legitimize it by proposing a Cabinet meeting that gave it legal appearance.

North Korea's constitutional revisions fundamentally alter the government's social contract with citizens while confirming state control over the economy.
NK Pro analysis examines North Korea's purported constitutional changes, identifying major shifts in the government's role in society and its relationship with citizens. The revisions alter the foundation of the socialist economic system, adding new clauses that broaden or make less specific state economic responsibilities. Analysis suggests the changes represent a transformation of the social contract between the DPRK state and its people.
So what: Contributes to understanding how Kim Jong Un is reshaping domestic governance structures beyond the foreign policy shift away from unification.
