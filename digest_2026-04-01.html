Top Stories

Seoul stocks jump over 8% amid hopes for Middle East conflict end

South Korean stocks surged Wednesday, led by gains in chipmakers and shipbuilders as investors bet on Middle East de-escalation. KOSPI closed at 5,478.70, up 3.82% from previous session, with Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix posting double-digit gains. Market rally coincided with announcement of Korea's inclusion in World Government Bond Index.

So what: Directly affects foreign investment flows and BOK monetary policy considerations at April 24 rate decision.

Yonhap English

Korea's monthly exports top $80 billion mark for first time in March

South Korea's monthly exports breached the $80 billion threshold for the first time, reaching a record high in March 2026. Semiconductor exports led the surge alongside strong automotive and petrochemical shipments to global markets. Government launched inter-ministerial task force to sustain export momentum amid global economic uncertainties.

So what: Supports government's 2026 GDP growth targets and strengthens case for currency stability measures.

Yonhap English

Lee, Prabowo agree to elevate bilateral ties, expand defense cooperation

President Lee Jae-myung and Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto agreed to strengthen bilateral partnership and deepen defense industry collaboration during Seoul summit. Leaders signed agreements on defense technology transfer and joint development programs. Indonesia remains crucial partner for Korea's KF-21 fighter jet program and naval export initiatives.

So what: Advances Korea's defense export strategy and strengthens Indo-Pacific partnership network.

All 12 peace-themed DMZ walking trails to reopen this year
Government announced full reopening of all border walking routes near the Demilitarized Zone for 2026. The trails have been partially closed since COVID-era restrictions began in 2020. Decision reflects easing inter-Korean tensions and growing tourism demand for DMZ experiences.

Office of US Trade Representative flagged South Korea's AI government procurement practices and digital platform regulations as trade barriers. Report also cited forced labor compliance issues in Korean supply chains. Government pledged continued consultation with Washington over identified concerns.
