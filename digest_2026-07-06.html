DPRK

Kim Jong Un observes cruise missile test from destroyer Kang Kon, orders commissioning within two months

Kim Jong Un personally observed a strategic cruise missile launch and additional weapons tests from the naval destroyer Kang Kon on July 5, according to KCNA, and ordered the vessel commissioned within two months. The Kang Kon is the sister ship of the Choe Hyon, which Kim commissioned on June 25 — meaning DPRK is on track to field two 5,000-ton destroyers within weeks of each other, following the June 25 Workers' Party plenum directive to build two warships annually for five years. The Moneycontrol report citing state media adds that Kim set a specific two-month commissioning deadline.

So what: Directly advances the DPRK naval expansion timeline announced at the June 25 plenum; watch for KCNA publication of the full plenum resolution and any 10,000-ton missile cruiser construction announcement ahead of the July 27 armistice anniversary.

Pattern: The CSIS provocations database records a July 7, 2020 anti-ship cruise missile test fired from a ship in waters between Korea and Japan; the Kang Kon test continues a pattern of DPRK naval missile demonstrations from surface vessels.

Reuters, NK News, Korea Times, AP — Jul 5-6, 2026
NK-Russia-China

NK Pro satellite imagery: North Korea-China border smuggling routes resume after months-long shutdown

NK Pro analysis of satellite imagery shows over a dozen makeshift smuggling routes across the North Korea-China border — used to send vehicles and other goods into the DPRK — have reactivated in recent weeks after going quiet since late 2025. The apparent shutdown by early 2026 came months after Asiapress had reported on the routes. The resumption coincides with the March 2026 restart of cross-border passenger rail at Sinuiju-Dandong.

So what: Relevant to UN sanctions monitors assessing DPRK import channels for dual-use goods and to the Section 301 China-origin goods investigation (USTR, launched Mar 11, 2026) tracking goods transshipped through third countries.

Lee Jae-myung approval ticks up 0.5 pts to 47% after six-week decline — 'three mega projects' credited, KOSPI and won decline cited as drag

President Lee Jae-myung's approval rating rose 0.5 percentage points to 47% in a Realmeter poll commissioned by EKN, ending a six-week consecutive decline. Disapproval stood at 49.2%, meaning Lee remains net-negative.

So what: The KOSPI's -4.09% decline on July 6 and weak KRW (1,530.89) may further complicate Lee's approval trajectory heading into the August 17 Democratic Party convention.
