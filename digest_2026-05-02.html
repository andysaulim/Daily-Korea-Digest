Top Stories

Moscow, Beijing back Pyongyang at UN sanctions meeting

Russia and China provided diplomatic support for North Korea during a UN Security Council sanctions committee meeting, blocking new enforcement measures and challenging existing restrictions. The coordinated backing demonstrates deepening trilateral alignment as Moscow and Beijing shield Pyongyang from international pressure over its weapons programs and Russia arms transfers.

So what: Directly affects UN sanctions enforcement mechanisms ahead of next IAEA Board meeting in June

Dong-A English

Seoul signals careful assessment after US moves to raise EU tariffs

South Korea will carefully assess the Trump administration's moves to increase tariffs on European Union countries, signaling concern about potential spillover effects on Korea-US trade arrangements. The cautious response comes as Seoul navigates its $350 billion investment commitment while managing existing Section 122 surcharges and sector-specific tariffs under the strategic trade deal.

So what: May influence Korea's approach to Section 301 investigations launched March 11

Yonhap

Hanwha builds arms empire as Ukraine, Iran wars drive demand

Hanwha Group's defense revenues have tripled since 2022 as global conflicts drive unprecedented demand for South Korean weapons systems, with new contracts for K9 howitzers, Redback infantry vehicles, and naval systems. The conglomerate is expanding production capacity through acquisitions including Philly Shipyard ($5 billion commitment) while positioning Korea as a top-five global arms exporter alongside traditional defense giants.

So what: Supports Korea's defense export target of $20 billion by 2027

CSIS
A 'cold peace' framework offers the most realistic path for managing North Korea tensions given failed denuclearization efforts and entrenched positions
CSIS Korea Chair analysis proposes shifting from denuclearization-focused diplomacy to a 'cold peace' management strategy with North Korea. The framework would prioritize risk reduction, crisis management mechanisms, and limited engagement on humanitarian issues while maintaining deterrence. Historical precedents from Cold War stability mechanisms between nuclear powers provide templates for reducing miscalculation risks without legitimizing North Korea's nuclear status.
So what: Offers alternative policy framework as denuclearization talks remain stalled

American Enterprise Institute analysis argues that North Korea has achieved irreversible nuclear weapons capability, necessitating a fundamental shift in US policy approach. So what: Challenges core assumptions underlying current US-ROK alliance approach to DPRK
