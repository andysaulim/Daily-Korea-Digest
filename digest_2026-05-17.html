Top Stories

North Korean women's soccer team arrives in South Korea for regional tournament

A North Korean women's soccer team arrived in South Korea on Sunday for the first time in almost eight years, with 27 players and 12 coaching staff from Pyongyang's Naegohyang Women's Football Club crossing the border. The delegation received a rapturous reception as they emerged for the highly anticipated inter-Korean match. The visit marks the first DPRK sporting delegation to enter the South since 2018 Winter Olympics engagement.

NK News

PM issues warning over planned Samsung Electronics strike, vows emergency arbitration

Prime Minister Kim Min-seok on Sunday welcomed planned Samsung Electronics labor-management dialogue but warned the government will pursue emergency arbitration if strike action causes damage to the national economy. The National Samsung Electronics Union has threatened indefinite strike action over pay disputes and working conditions. The warning reflects government concerns about potential semiconductor supply chain disruptions amid global chip competition.

Japanese PM to receive near state-visit honors during planned visit to S. Korea

Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi will be welcomed with near state-visit honors during her planned visit to South Korea, the presidential office announced Sunday. The high-level protocol treatment signals Seoul's commitment to strengthening bilateral ties with Tokyo under new leadership. The visit represents continued momentum in Japan-Korea diplomatic normalization following years of historical disputes.
