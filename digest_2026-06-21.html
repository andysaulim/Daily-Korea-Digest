Ukraine assesses improved accuracy of North Korean ballistic missiles deployed by Russia

Ukraine's military has assessed that North Korean ballistic missiles used by Russia in the conflict have demonstrated improved accuracy, Kyodo News reports, marking a qualitative development in the DPRK-Russia weapons transfer relationship. Kim Jong Un in his June 17 statement pledged 'full support and solidarity' to Russia's military operation in Ukraine, and the CSIS tracker records 64 voyages delivering approximately 15,800 containers from Rajin to Russian ports since September 2023.

Election-fraud protests reach 17th day with 34,000 at Seoul venue, young adults majority

Protests demanding a rerun of the June 3 local elections marred by ballot shortages continued for a 17th consecutive day on Sunday, with the Seoul city government estimating 34,000 people at the SK Olympic Handball Gymnasium in Songpa — the ballot counting site used for the elections — with those in their 20s and 30s comprising more than half. The National Election Commission has apologized for ballot shortages that temporarily suspended voting at 26 polling stations but says the irregularities do not warrant a rerun. The sustained youth-majority protests create a political pressure point for the Lee administration entering its second year.

So what: The outcome of NEC's formal review and any court challenge directly affects the Democratic Party's consolidated political position ahead of next scheduled legislative elections.
