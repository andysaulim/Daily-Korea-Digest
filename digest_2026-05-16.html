Top Stories

Trump says U.S. in contact with Kim Jong Un amid China state visit

President Trump confirmed Friday that the United States maintains communication channels with North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, speaking to reporters during his Beijing state visit wrap-up. Trump emphasized his 'very good relationship' with Kim while revealing he discussed North Korea issues with Xi Jinping during their two-day summit. The disclosure comes as speculation mounts about potential renewed U.S.-DPRK diplomatic engagement following the failed 2019 Hanoi summit.

So what: Creates diplomatic space ahead of potential US-DPRK engagement window.

Pattern: Trump-Xi discussed North Korea during their summit, similar to coordination before the 2018 Singapore summit.

NK News

Samsung chairman calls for company unity amid looming strike

Samsung Electronics Chairman Lee Jae-yong urged company unity Saturday as the tech giant faces its first potential strike in its 55-year history. The National Samsung Electronics Union, representing 28,000 workers, agreed to resume wage talks Monday after management replaced its chief negotiator, but maintains its strike authorization. The labor dispute centers on wage increases and bonus calculations as Samsung navigates intense competition in the global semiconductor market.

So what: First Samsung strike could disrupt semiconductor production amid US-China tech competition.

Yonhap

Son Heung-min off to 4th World Cup as S. Korean squad released

Tottenham captain Son Heung-min will lead South Korea's 26-man World Cup squad for his fourth consecutive tournament, with German-born Jeong Woo-yeong (Union Berlin) receiving his first call-up. The squad features a blend of European-based stars and K-League talent as Korea prepares to face UEFA Playoff D winner in Guadalajara on June 11. Coach Hong Myung-bo emphasized empowering players during extended preparation camps beginning next week.

So what: Squad selection shapes Korea's 2026 World Cup campaign hosted in North America.
