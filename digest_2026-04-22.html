Foreign Affairs
Kim Jong Un has achieved strategic victory by building nuclear capabilities that ensure regime survival while expanding international partnerships with Russia and China.
Jung Pak argues that North Korea under Kim Jong Un has fundamentally won the strategic competition by developing credible nuclear deterrent capabilities that guarantee regime survival. The piece examines how Kim has leveraged nuclear weapons development to secure partnerships with Russia and China while deterring US military action. Pak contends that traditional policy approaches toward North Korea must be reconsidered given this new strategic reality.
So what: Challenges current US policy assumptions about coercion and engagement strategies toward North Korea.
Foreign Affairs
North Korea's deepening military and technological partnerships with Russia and China create unprecedented threats to US and allied security interests.
Oriana Skylar Mastro examines how North Korea's expanding cooperation with Russia and China in military technology and weapons development fundamentally alters regional security dynamics. The analysis focuses on weapons transfers, technological sharing, and coordinated opposition to US influence. Mastro argues that this trilateral cooperation represents a qualitatively different challenge requiring new deterrence strategies.
So what: Informs US alliance strategy and regional deterrence planning for Northeast Asia.
Foreign Affairs
The United States should accept North Korea as a nuclear power and pursue a 'cold peace' strategy focused on arms control rather than denuclearization.
CSIS Korea Chair Victor Cha advocates for accepting North Korea's nuclear status and pursuing arms control agreements rather than complete denuclearization. The piece outlines a 'cold peace' approach that emphasizes stability through deterrence and limited engagement. Cha argues that decades of denuclearization efforts have failed and policy should adapt to nuclear reality.
So what: Directly challenges current US policy framework requiring complete North Korean denuclearization.

The Iran conflict demonstrates that South Korea's security extends beyond the peninsula and requires alliance adaptation to global contingencies affecting Korean interests.
The piece examines how Middle East tensions and Strait of Hormuz disruptions directly impact South Korean energy security and economic stability. It argues that the US-ROK alliance must evolve beyond its traditional Korean Peninsula focus to address global threats affecting Korean national interests.
