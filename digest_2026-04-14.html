Top Stories

N. Korea's Kim observes test-firing of cruise, anti-warship missiles from key destroyer

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un oversaw test-firing of cruise missiles and anti-ship missiles from the destroyer Choe Hyon on the country's west coast, state media reported. The tests demonstrated the warship's mixed missile capabilities including surface-to-surface cruise missiles and ship-to-ship anti-warship missiles in what KCNA described as a key enhancement of naval strike power. Kim expressed satisfaction with the destroyer's combat readiness and ordered continued development of sea-based nuclear deterrent capabilities.

So what: Timing one day before Kim Il Sung's birthday suggests military display coordination with DPRK's most important holiday

KCNA via Yonhap

IMF maintains S. Korea's 2026 growth outlook at 1.9 pct, raises inflation forecast to 2.5 pct

The International Monetary Fund kept South Korea's 2026 economic growth forecast unchanged at 1.9% while raising its inflation projection to 2.5% from 2.3% amid persistent price pressures. The IMF cited resilient domestic demand and recovering exports but warned of downside risks from Middle East tensions affecting energy costs and supply chains. The revised inflation estimate exceeds the Bank of Korea's 2% target range, potentially complicating monetary policy decisions ahead of the April 24 rate meeting.

So what: Higher inflation forecast may influence BOK's April 24 rate decision amid debate over policy accommodation

IMF World Economic Outlook

Korea, Poland highlight defense cooperation as 'driving force' of bilateral ties

South Korea and Poland elevated their strategic partnership during high-level consultations, with both sides emphasizing defense cooperation as the primary driver of expanding bilateral relations. Polish officials praised Korea's delivery schedule for K2 tanks and K9 howitzers as ahead of contracted timelines, while Seoul highlighted Poland's role as a gateway to broader European defense markets. The partnership has generated over $20 billion in defense contracts since 2022, making Poland Korea's largest European arms customer.

So what: Success with Poland validates Seoul's strategy of leveraging Ukraine war demand to expand European defense market share

Trump nominates former Rep. Michelle Park Steel as US ambassador to S. Korea
US President Donald Trump nominated former California Representative Michelle Park Steel as ambassador to South Korea, ending a yearlong vacancy in the key diplomatic post. Steel, a Korean American Republican, served in Congress from 2021-2023 representing Orange County and would require Senate confirmation to fill the role left vacant since January 2025. The nomination comes as Washington and Seoul navigate trade disputes and alliance coordination amid Middle East tensions.
Business · Hankyoreh English
South Korea surges to world's No. 4 arms exporter in 2025
South Korea rose to become the world's fourth-largest arms exporter in 2025, jumping from eighth place the previous year driven by major defense contracts with Poland and other European allies. The surge reflects Seoul's success in marketing K2 Black Panther tanks, K9 Thunder howitzers, and FA-50 fighter aircraft amid heightened European defense spending following Russia's invasion of Ukraine. Poland alone has contracted for over $20 billion in Korean weapons systems as part of its military modernization program.
US-Korea · Yonhap English
S. Korea to attend multilateral meeting on Strait of Hormuz this week
South Korea will attend a multilateral meeting this week on maritime security in the Strait of Hormuz as tensions escalate between the US and Iran following recent attacks on shipping lanes. The meeting comes after Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back told lawmakers Seoul has not received formal US requests for military support in the strategic waterway through which 20% of global oil transits. Seoul is balancing alliance obligations with economic interests given Iran's role as a regional energy supplier.
China-Korea · Yonhap English
N. Korean leader's 1st public reference to 'one-China' principle points to closer bilateral ties
North Korean state media reported leader Kim Jong Un's first public reference to the 'one-China' principle during Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi's recent Pyongyang visit, signaling deeper alignment with Beijing on Taiwan policy. Seoul's unification ministry assessed the statement as evidence of strengthening DPRK-China coordination ahead of potential shifts in regional dynamics. The endorsement marks a departure from Pyongyang's previous careful neutrality on the Taiwan issue despite its alliance with China.
US-Korea · Hankyoreh English
Trump lashes out at Korea again over Iran: 'These people haven't helped us'
President Trump criticized South Korea's response to the Iran crisis, stating 'These people haven't helped us' during remarks to reporters about allied support for US Middle East policy. The comments came as Seoul faces pressure to contribute militarily to Strait of Hormuz operations while maintaining economic ties with Iran and managing domestic opposition to overseas deployments. Trump's criticism echoes previous frustrations with Korean cost-sharing and alliance burden-sharing arrangements.
