Seoul court scraps Homeplus rehabilitation, pushing $5.7B MBK revamp toward bankruptcy

A Seoul court terminated Homeplus's court-led rehabilitation process, effectively ending MBK Partners' bid to restructure the retailer and pushing it toward bankruptcy. The collapse of the $5.7B revamp is the largest private equity restructuring failure in recent Korean retail history and leaves thousands of employees and creditors exposed.

So what: Directly affects creditor recovery proceedings and the broader Korean private equity market's appetite for large-cap leveraged buyouts.

Samsung seen in talks to manufacture custom AI chips for Anthropic

Ruling DPK expresses 'strong regret' over US House Judiciary report alleging Korea discriminates against Coupang

The ruling Democratic Party of Korea called a U.S. House Judiciary Committee interim staff report alleging South Korea discriminates against Coupang and other American firms "seriously distorted" and reliant on "one-sided claims." Party spokesperson Jang Yoon-mi said the report relies on unverified data and criticized Coupang's conduct in its own data leak case. The report claimed Korea subjected US-owned firms to coercive investigations, overly burdensome regulatory requirements, and excessive fines.

So what: The congressional report and Seoul's pushback land 20 days before the Section 122 surcharge expires, adding regulatory friction to the US-Korea bilateral trade relationship at a sensitive moment.

China-Korea · Nikkei Asia
South Korea's tungsten mine revival seeks China-free supply chain
Nikkei Asia reports that South Korea is reviving domestic tungsten mining as part of a broader drive to reduce dependence on Chinese critical mineral supply chains. Tungsten is a key input for defense and semiconductor manufacturing; China currently dominates global supply. The effort aligns with the Korea-US Strategic Investment Corporation's critical minerals mandate and the Korea Zinc Crucible Metals deal (Pentagon 40% JV stake, $7.4B) announced in December 2025.
