North Korea's powerhouse women footballers are in Seoul to fight for title

North Korean women's football club Naegohyang made history by competing in and winning the AFC Women's Champions League final in Seoul, marking the first time a North Korean sports team has played competitively in South Korea since 2019. The team defeated Brazil's Beleza 2-0 in front of a packed stadium, with South Korean fans cheering for both sides in a rare moment of sporting diplomacy. The victory demonstrates North Korea's continued excellence in women's football despite years of international isolation and raises questions about prize money transfers given financial sanctions.

Lee vows to carry on ex-President Roh's legacy at memorial ceremony

President Lee Jae-myung pledged to continue former President Roh Moo-hyun's progressive legacy during a memorial ceremony marking the 17th anniversary of Roh's death in Bongha village. Lee promised to build a society 'where success is possible without privilege' and 'balanced development allows both the capital and provinces to prosper,' echoing Roh's political philosophy. The ceremony drew thousands of supporters and marked Lee's first attendance as president, signaling his administration's commitment to Roh's vision of participatory democracy and social justice.

He died in a Korean War prison camp. Now Emil Kapaun could become US military's first saint
Father Emil Kapaun, a US Army chaplain who died in a North Korean POW camp in 1951, has moved closer to becoming the first US military saint after Vatican approval of key documentation. Kapaun was captured at the Battle of Unsan and died seven months later on May 23, 1951, after ministering to fellow POWs despite severe maltreatment. His potential canonization would mark a historic milestone for US military chaplaincy and Korean War remembrance, coinciding with the upcoming 75th anniversary of the conflict.
