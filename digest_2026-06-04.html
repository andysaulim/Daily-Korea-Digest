Kim Jong Un inspects new nuclear plant, calls for exponential weapons expansion

US-Korea

Lee's Democratic Party sweeps local elections but loses crucial Seoul contest

President Lee Jae-myung's ruling Democratic Party captured the majority of mayoral and gubernatorial races in Wednesday's nationwide local elections, winning an estimated 12 of 16 metropolitan and provincial posts. However, the party failed to unseat Seoul Mayor Oh Se-hoon of the opposition People Power Party, who secured reelection by a razor-thin 49.15% to 48.13% margin despite exit polls predicting his defeat. The mixed results provide both validation for Lee's presidency and a symbolic setback in the nation's political capital.

So what: Seoul loss limits Lee's mandate heading into 2027 presidential election cycle

Korean won worst performer among Asian peers, tests new crisis-era low

The Korean won weakened to 1,550 per dollar, testing levels not seen since the 2009 financial crisis and marking the worst performance among major Asian currencies. Finance Minister vowed government response to excessive volatility as the currency's 2.58% decline today reflected broader Middle East tensions and dollar strength. The weakness compounds inflation pressures ahead of next week's central bank policy meeting.

So what: Triggers potential BOK intervention discussion at June 10 monetary policy board meeting
