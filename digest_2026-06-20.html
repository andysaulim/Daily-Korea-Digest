Kim Yo Jong warns G7 'inviting disaster' by calling for DPRK denuclearization

Kim Yo Jong declared in a KCNA statement that the G7's call for DPRK denuclearization is 'inviting disaster,' framing nuclear arms as a 'means of self-defense' and denouncing denuclearization as a 'line of no retreat.' The statement directly rejects language from the most recent G7 communique and represents the most explicit doctrinal hardening of Pyongyang's nuclear posture in recent KCNA output. Kim Jong Un remains absent from public view for 13 consecutive days, with no party plenum yet announced.

So what: Directly conditions the diplomatic environment ahead of any US-DPRK contact signaled by Trump's hints at a new approach to North Korea's nuclear program.

Trump asks if South Korea can quickly build 10 naval vessels

President Trump publicly asked whether South Korea could rapidly construct 10 naval vessels, a direct signal of US interest in Korean shipbuilding capacity that intersects with the $150B MASGA shipbuilding component of the US-Korea $350B investment pledge. The query follows existing commitments from HD Hyundai/Cerberus Maritime ($5B) and Hanwha Ocean ($5B) for US shipyard modernization under the Oct 2025 summit framework. The ROK-US Strategic Investment Corporation established by the National Assembly's Mar 12, 2026 Special Investment Act (226-8-8) provides the institutional structure for any formal procurement arrangement.

So what: Bears directly on the $150B MASGA shipbuilding bucket of the $350B pledge, where only $10B in firm commitments has been announced against a $150B target.
