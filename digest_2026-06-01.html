S. Korea's monthly exports up 53 pct to record high of US$87.8 bln in May

South Korea's exports surged 53 percent year-on-year to a record $87.8 billion in May, with semiconductor shipments hitting an all-time high of $37.2 billion driven by AI chip demand. The export boom marks the eighth consecutive month of growth and reflects Korea's central position in the global AI supply chain as memory chip prices recover. China remained the top destination at $12.4 billion followed by the United States at $11.2 billion, while exports to all major markets showed double-digit growth.

So what: Directly impacts BOK's July 10 rate decision as strong export growth provides room to focus on inflation

Lee calls for all-out efforts for rescue operations after Hanwha Aerospace factory explosion

President Lee Jae-myung ordered government agencies to mobilize all available resources for rescue operations after a deadly explosion at Hanwha Aerospace's Daejeon facility, with Defense Minister Ahn Gyu-back pledging full ministry cooperation in the investigation. The industrial accident at one of Korea's major defense contractors prompted political parties to scale back local election campaigning ahead of the June 3 vote. Hanwha Aerospace, a key supplier for ROK military systems and part of the $350 billion US investment pledge, vowed a thorough investigation into the cause.

Ex-President Yoon made preparations for martial law since late 2023: special counsel

Special counsel investigators determined that former President Yoon Suk Yeol began preparations for martial law declaration in late 2023, significantly earlier than previously known, based on evidence gathered during the probe. Former National Intelligence Service chief Cho Tae-yong was questioned regarding his suspected role in the planning, as the investigation expands to include senior security officials from the previous administration. The revelations could impact public sentiment ahead of June 3 local elections as voters assess the current administration's democratic credentials against the backdrop of the unprecedented constitutional crisis.
