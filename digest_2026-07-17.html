Bank of Korea begins rate-hike cycle, raising benchmark to 2.50%

The Bank of Korea raised its benchmark rate to 2.50%, beginning a rate-hike cycle for the first time since 2023 to curb inflation and household debt, according to Dong-A Ilbo. Bloomberg analysis assessed South Korea's economy as 'too hot for one rate hike,' projecting additional tightening ahead. The KOSPI fell 6.46% (as of Jul 16), compounded by Korea's financial regulator tightening minimum deposit requirements for single-stock leveraged ETFs amid wild swings in chip-sector names.

So what — The rate-hike cycle directly affects the cost of Korea's record household debt load and the ROK government's fiscal posture ahead of the July 24 Section 122 surcharge expiry.

US-Korea

Lee approval rating edges down to 52% for second consecutive week — Gallup Korea

President Lee Jae-myung's approval rating fell to 52% in a Gallup Korea survey conducted July 15-17 (1,003 respondents aged 18+), down 1 percentage point from the prior week and 2 points from the 54% recorded in the July 3 poll. Disapproval rose 2 points on-week to 37%; diplomacy (roughly 20% of respondents) and the economy (19%) were the top cited reasons for positive assessment. The rating marks a consecutive weekly decline from the administration's recent high.

So what — The two-week slide in approval enters the digest cycle one week before the July 24 Section 122 surcharge expiry — a trade policy inflection point that directly affects Lee's economic credibility.
