Top Stories

Seoul stocks close at new record high of 6,615.03

The KOSPI shattered records Monday, surging 6.37% to close at 6,615.03 points, driven by an AI-led tech rally and optimistic corporate earnings expectations. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix led gains as investors bet on continued semiconductor demand growth, while power equipment stocks also rallied on data center expansion prospects. The benchmark index has now set fresh records for three consecutive sessions.

So what: Record market valuations strengthen Korea's position ahead of potential MSCI developed market upgrade decision in June 2026.

Yonhap

North Korea opens museum to honour soldiers killed fighting for Russia

North Korea inaugurated a memorial museum for its troops killed in Russia's war against Ukraine, marking the regime's first official acknowledgment of combat casualties. Kim Jong Un attended the ceremony alongside Russian Defense Minister Andrei Beloussov and State Duma speaker Vyacheslav Volodin, underscoring deepening military ties. The memorial represents a significant escalation in North Korea's public commitment to supporting Russia's military operations.

So what: Memorial opening signals North Korea's long-term commitment to Russia partnership beyond initial troop deployments.

Pattern: First time since Korean War (1950-53) that North Korea has officially commemorated troops killed in foreign combat operations.

FT

20-yr prison term sought for ex-justice minister in martial law case

Special prosecutors requested a 20-year prison sentence for former Justice Minister Han Dong-hoon over his role in the December 2025 martial law declaration. Prosecutors argued Han played a central role in planning unconstitutional measures and mobilizing prosecutors to support the failed coup attempt. The case represents the most severe sentence sought yet among former Yoon administration officials charged in the martial law scandal.

So what: Sentencing scheduled for May could set precedent for upcoming trials of other senior Yoon administration officials.
