Energy

Seoul begins consultations with US and Iran on Hormuz transit after peace deal

South Korea initiated consultations with the United States and Iran on restoring safe shipping through the Strait of Hormuz following the US-Iran ceasefire agreement announced June 15. The KOSPI surged 7.78% on Tuesday — closing above 8,700 for a fourth consecutive session — as Brent crude fell 11.43% to $80.05 on deal optimism. Seoul's energy security calculus is directly tied to Hormuz transit: South Korea imports the bulk of its crude oil from Gulf producers whose exports route through the strait.

So what: Restored Hormuz transit would reduce South Korea's energy import costs and ease inflationary pressure ahead of the July 10 BOK rate decision.

Hanwha Aerospace to acquire additional KAI shares for 500 billion won

Hanwha Aerospace announced Tuesday it will acquire additional shares in Korea Aerospace Industries (KAI) for 500 billion won, deepening its stake in South Korea's sole fixed-wing aircraft manufacturer. The move consolidates Hanwha Group's position at the center of South Korea's domestic defense industrial base at a moment when Seoul is pursuing simultaneous KF-21 fighter certification and nuclear submarine development.

So what: Directly affects the structure of Korea's defense industrial base as the KF-21 program moves toward operational deployment and the nuclear submarine roadmap advances.

Rival parties agree to 45-day parliamentary probe on election ballot shortage

South Korea's Democratic Party and People Power Party reached a bipartisan agreement on Tuesday to launch a 45-day parliamentary investigation into ballot shortages at counting centers during the June 3 local elections. The Board of Audit and Inspection is also considering an accounting audit of the election watchdog.

So what: The probe's timeline runs through mid-July, overlapping with the Section 122 tariff expiry on July 24 and potential National Assembly action on trade and investment legislation.

Russia using North Korean missile tech to upgrade its own weapons, Kyiv says
