Korean conglomerates commit 312 trillion won to southeast in AI and SMR push

South Korean conglomerates announced a combined 312 trillion won in investment commitments to the southeastern region, targeting artificial intelligence and small modular reactor sectors. The announcement came alongside President Lee Jae-myung's pledge to create an 'aerospace industry belt' along the southern coast, with SK Telecom alone committing 140 trillion won for hyperscale AI data centers in Yeongnam and Hyundai Motor Group pledging 42 trillion won in mobility and physical AI. The regional investment package represents a major industrial policy push timed to ongoing US-Korea trade negotiations and the Section 122 surcharge expiry on July 24.

So what: Directly relevant to the US-Korea $350B investment framework negotiations and MOTIE's industrial policy posture ahead of the July 24 Section 122 expiry.

Lee to attend NATO summit in Turkey, visit Mongolia next week

President Lee Jae-myung will travel to Turkey for the NATO summit next week and then to Mongolia for bilateral talks with the Mongolian president — his first NATO summit as ROK head of state since taking office in June 2025. South Korean Marine Corps personnel are simultaneously participating in multinational peacekeeping drills in Mongolia.

So what: NATO summit provides Lee's first major multilateral platform to coordinate on DPRK policy with European and allied partners, with the July 27 armistice anniversary 24 days out.
