Top Stories

South Korea posts largest-ever monthly current account surplus in March

South Korea recorded a $20.8 billion current account surplus in March, the highest monthly figure on record, driven by robust export performance and recovering global demand. The Bank of Korea data showed the goods surplus reached $18.2 billion while the services account posted a smaller deficit than previous months. The record surplus reflects Korea's strengthening export competitiveness in semiconductors, automobiles, and industrial machinery amid global economic recovery.

So what: Supports BOK's cautious monetary policy stance ahead of the May 22 rate decision

BOK · May 8

Seoul shares soar to fresh record high of 7,498 despite Middle East tensions

The KOSPI closed at an all-time high of 7,498.00, extending gains for the seventh consecutive session despite renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East affecting global oil markets. Foreign institutional investors continued net buying of Korean equities, purchasing $2.1 billion worth of shares as the market showed resilience to external volatility. Technology and automotive stocks led the rally with Samsung Electronics and Hyundai Motor posting significant gains on strong earnings expectations.

So what: Reinforces foreign confidence in Korean assets ahead of June 3 local elections

Pattern: KOSPI has gained 12.06% this week amid global market uncertainty

KRX · May 8

North Korea to deploy new long-range artillery along border with South Korea

KCNA reported that North Korea will deploy new long-range artillery systems along the inter-Korean border this year, capable of striking Seoul and surrounding metropolitan areas. Kim Jong Un inspected the artillery production facility and ordered mass production of the weapons system, which reportedly has enhanced range and precision compared to existing border artillery. The deployment announcement comes amid heightened military tensions and North Korea's continued weapons development despite international sanctions.

So what: Directly threatens Seoul metropolitan area with 25 million residents

Pattern: First KCNA artillery deployment announcement since constitutional changes dropping unification language

US-Korea · Yonhap
Ruling party fails to push through constitutional amendment amid opposition boycott
The Democratic Party's attempt to advance constitutional amendment legislation collapsed as People Power Party members boycotted key procedural votes in the National Assembly. The amendment required a two-thirds majority but fell significantly short as opposition lawmakers refused to participate, dealing a major blow to President Lee Jae-myung's reform agenda. The setback highlights deepening political polarization just weeks before nationwide local elections scheduled for June 3.
US-Korea · Yonhap
US trade court rules against Trump's 10% global tariffs
A US trade court issued a landmark ruling Thursday blocking President Trump's proposed 10% global tariff system, citing procedural violations and constitutional concerns over executive trade authority. The decision affects South Korea among other trading partners and could force the administration to seek congressional approval for broad-based tariff increases. Legal experts suggest the ruling may reshape US trade policy enforcement mechanisms and provide relief for Korean exporters facing mounting trade barriers.
Energy · Yonhap
Fourth South Korean oil tanker successfully transits Red Sea
A South Korean vessel completed safe passage through the Red Sea shipping corridor despite ongoing regional tensions and Iranian threats to commercial maritime traffic. The successful transit marks the fourth Korean tanker to navigate the critical route this month as Seoul maintains essential energy supply chains. Government officials emphasized continued coordination with international naval forces to protect Korean commercial shipping in the strategically vital waterway.
DPRK · Dong-A English
South Korea tightens drone and draft evasion laws
The National Assembly approved comprehensive legislation strengthening penalties for unauthorized drone operations and military service evasion as security concerns intensify. New drone regulations impose stricter licensing requirements and heavier fines following North Korean surveillance drone incursions over Seoul and military facilities. The draft evasion measures target wealthy individuals using overseas residency to avoid mandatory military service, reflecting growing public frustration with inequality in national defense obligations.
