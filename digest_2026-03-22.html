Top Stories

South Korea Names BIS Official as Next Central Bank Chief

President Lee Jae Myung nominated Shin Hyun-song, an economic adviser at the Bank for International Settlements, as the next Bank of Korea governor amid escalating Middle East tensions threatening energy security. Shin pledged 'balanced' monetary policy as the won weakened past 1,500 per dollar and inflation pressures mount from oil prices above $107.

So what: Confirmation hearings expected before April monetary policy meeting.

Pattern: First BOK governor nomination from BIS since 2010.

WSJ · Mar 22

N. Korea's Kim reappointed as president of state affairs

North Korean leader Kim Jong Un was reappointed as president of state affairs at the new Supreme People's Assembly meeting Sunday, with his daughter's prominent appearances at recent military events fueling succession speculation. The parliament session is expected to revise the constitution to reflect Kim's January order designating South Korea as the 'primary foe.'

So what: Constitutional revision could formalize hostile stance before April 15 holiday.

Yonhap · Mar 23

S. Korea's exports surge 50.4% in first 20 days of March

South Korea's exports jumped 50.4 percent year-on-year in the first 20 days of March, driven by semiconductor demand as AI applications expand globally. The surge marks a sharp recovery from the 2025 export slump and could support GDP growth above the Bank of Korea's 1.5% forecast.

So what: March expected to post first $60B+ monthly export total since 2022.

Yonhap · Mar 23

Gov't agrees 25 tln-won supplementary budget for Mideast crisis

The government and ruling Democratic Party agreed on a 25 trillion won supplementary budget to address economic impacts from the Middle East crisis, with oil above $107 and shipping routes threatened. The package includes energy subsidies, support for affected industries, and strategic reserve releases.

So what: Assembly passage expected April 10 before Q2 energy price adjustments.

Pattern: Largest crisis-response supplementary budget since COVID-19 in 2020.
