Top Stories

Kim Jong-un reappointed president of State Affairs Commission at SPA session

North Korean leader Kim Jong-un was reappointed as president of the State Affairs Commission during the first session of the 15th Supreme People's Assembly. The rubber-stamp parliament session confirms Kim's continued grip on power through formal state channels. The appointment ceremony followed standard DPRK protocol with no reported opposition or procedural changes.

So what: Demonstrates regime stability ahead of potential April 15 Kim Il Sung birthday provocations and ongoing NK-Russia military cooperation

Pattern: Follows the same five-year reappointment cycle established since the SAC replaced the National Defense Commission in 2016

Yonhap English

South Korea names BIS official Shin Hyun-song as central bank chief

President Lee Jae Myung nominated Shin Hyun-song, a Bank for International Settlements economist, to lead the Bank of Korea as the current governor's term expires. Shin pledged a 'balanced' monetary policy approach balancing growth and inflation concerns amid global economic uncertainty. The appointment requires National Assembly confirmation and signals continuity in BOK policy direction.

So what: Critical for Korea's monetary policy stance as regional inflation pressures mount and Middle East crisis affects energy costs

Pattern: First BOK governor change in four years, following the pattern of five-year terms established since central bank independence in 1998

WSJ Korea

Finance minister warns of prolonged Middle East crisis impact on Korea

Finance Minister Koo Yun-cheol called for preparations for an extended Middle East crisis affecting Korea's energy imports and supply chains. The government is developing contingency measures including strategic petroleum reserve releases and alternative supply routes. Korea imports approximately 70 percent of its crude oil from the Middle East region making it vulnerable to regional disruptions.

So what: Directly informs the 25 trillion won supplementary budget debate and emergency energy security measures under consideration

Seoul mandates gender pay gap disclosure to address wage inequality
South Korea will require companies to publicly report gender pay gaps as part of government efforts to address wage inequality between men and women. The mandate targets major employers and aims to increase transparency in corporate compensation practices. Korea maintains one of the largest gender wage gaps among developed economies at approximately 31 percent.
