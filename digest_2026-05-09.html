Top Stories

(LEAD) N. Korean troops march in Russia's Victory Day parade for 1st time

North Korean troops marched in Russia's Victory Day military parade in Red Square for the first time, marking a historic display of military cooperation between Pyongyang and Moscow. The participation comes as North Korea has deployed troops to fight alongside Russian forces in Ukraine and supplied weapons for Moscow's war effort. The parade appearance represents the most visible symbol yet of the deepening NK-Russia strategic partnership.

So what: On the agenda for the May 14-15 Trump-Xi Beijing summit discussions on regional security architecture

Pattern: Last year on May 9, 2025, Kim Jong-un visited the Russian embassy in Pyongyang to celebrate Victory Day

Yonhap English · May 9

(LEAD) N. Korea's Kim reaffirms commitment to deepening ties with Russia in Victory Day message

North Korean leader Kim Jong-un sent a congratulatory message to Vladimir Putin calling ties with Russia a top priority on Victory Day. Kim emphasized strengthening strategic cooperation and partnership between the two countries. The message accompanied the first-ever participation of North Korean troops in Moscow's Red Square parade.

Pattern: Kim visited the Russian embassy in Pyongyang with his daughter on Victory Day 2025

Yonhap English · May 9

(2nd LD) S. Korea, U.S. sign MOU to advance shipbuilding cooperation

South Korea and the United States signed a memorandum of understanding to advance bilateral shipbuilding cooperation, with President Trump saying "I love S. Korea" after the agreement. The MOU builds on the $150 billion shipbuilding component of Korea's $350 billion investment pledge. The agreement aims to strengthen supply chain resilience and industrial cooperation between the allies.
