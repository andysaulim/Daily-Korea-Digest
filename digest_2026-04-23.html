Top Stories

Lee's approval rating holds at record 69% for third straight poll

President Lee Jae-myung's approval rating remained at 69 percent in National Barometer Survey conducted Monday to Wednesday, marking third consecutive poll at this level. Negative assessment dropped 1 percentage point to 21 percent from previous survey. The 69 percent approval first reached in fourth week of March represents highest level since his February 2026 inauguration.

So what: Sustained peak approval provides political capital ahead of June 3 local elections, the first electoral test for Lee's administration.

Korea Times

Honda exits S. Korean car market after 23 years

Honda Korea announced withdrawal from South Korean automotive market after 23 years of operations, citing consistently poor sales performance. The Japanese automaker's exit reflects intensified competition from domestic manufacturers and changing consumer preferences in the Korean market.

Yonhap English

Hyundai Rotem wins $330M Vietnam unmanned train contract

Hyundai Rotem secured 491 billion won ($330 million) contract to supply unmanned trains for Vietnam's Ho Chi Minh City metro system. The deal includes 17 four-car train sets for the Ben Thanh-Suoi Tien line, with delivery scheduled through 2028.

So what: Contract signed during President Lee's Vietnam visit, demonstrating infrastructure cooperation outcomes from the bilateral summit.
