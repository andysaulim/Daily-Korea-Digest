Top Stories

Samsung workers warn of 30 trillion won loss from planned general strike

Unionized workers at Samsung Electronics warned of potential production losses up to 30 trillion won if their planned general strike proceeds. The union is demanding wage increases and better working conditions as the company faces intensifying competition in memory semiconductors and AI chips. Samsung's largest union represents thousands of workers at key production facilities including the Pyeongtaek campus.

So what: Strike could disrupt global semiconductor supply chains and impact Samsung's AI chip production commitments to major US tech companies.

Yonhap · Apr 17

Gasoline prices top 2,000 won per liter for first time in nearly 4 years

Average gasoline prices in South Korea reached 2,000 won per liter for the first time in 3 years and 9 months, driven by prolonged Middle East tensions and rising crude oil costs. Brent crude has surged above $88 per barrel as supply chain disruptions continue from the Iran conflict. The price milestone adds inflationary pressure on Korean consumers already facing elevated living costs.

So what: Higher energy costs threaten to undermine Bank of Korea's inflation targets and may influence the Apr 24 monetary policy decision.

Yonhap · Apr 17

HD Hyundai Heavy wins 674.7 billion won VLGC order in Middle East

HD Hyundai Heavy Industries secured a 674.7 billion won contract for very large gas carrier construction from a Middle East client. The order underscores continued demand for LNG transport capacity as global energy markets adapt to supply chain disruptions from regional conflicts. The shipbuilding win adds to South Korea's dominant position in specialized vessel construction.

So what: Reinforces Korea's strategic role in global energy infrastructure as Middle East suppliers seek reliable shipping partners.
