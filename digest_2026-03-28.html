Top Stories

Belarus and North Korea sign friendship treaty during Lukashenko visit

Belarus President Alexander Lukashenko signed a comprehensive partnership treaty with Kim Jong Un in Pyongyang during his first visit to North Korea, marked by a 21-gun salute and ceremonial welcome. The treaty formalizes cooperation between two key Russian allies as Belarus deepens ties with North Korea amid international isolation over support for Russia's Ukraine war. Lukashenko gifted Kim an assault rifle, saying it was 'just in case enemies appear,' while discussions reportedly included potential North Korean labor deployments to Belarus.

CNN

Russia warns Seoul of retaliatory measures over potential Ukraine arms aid

Russia's deputy foreign minister warned Moscow will take retaliatory measures if Seoul provides weapons to Ukraine, escalating diplomatic pressure on South Korea as it weighs lethal aid decisions. The warning comes as South Korean and Ukrainian foreign ministers agreed to handle North Korean prisoners of war captured in Ukraine according to humanitarian principles, confirming DPRK troop deployments. Seoul faces growing pressure from Western allies to provide defensive weapons systems while Russia threatens economic and diplomatic consequences.

Yonhap

Samsung Electronics unionized workers halt wage talks with management

Unionized workers at Samsung Electronics halted negotiations with management over wages and working conditions, raising the specter of the company's first major labor action. The breakdown affects roughly 30,000 unionized employees at the world's largest memory chipmaker, coming as Samsung faces intensifying competition in AI semiconductors and pressure to maintain production schedules. The union cited management's 'insincere attitude' in negotiations that have dragged on for months without agreement on wage increases or productivity bonuses.

Belarus President Alexander Lukashenko presented Kim Jong Un with an automatic rifle during his Pyongyang visit, saying it was 'just in case enemies appear.' The gift exchange highlighted deepening ties between two nations aligned with Russia, as Lukashenko's spokeswoman confirmed the leader is considering inviting Kim to Belarus and potentially visiting Washington to meet Trump.
US-Korea · CNA
What's pushing South Korea towards nuclear weapons?
Growing nuclear threats from North Korea and questions about US security commitments have reignited South Korea's nuclear weapons debate. Recent polls show over 70% public support for an indigenous nuclear capability, while policy experts debate whether Seoul should pursue its own deterrent or seek redeployment of US tactical nuclear weapons withdrawn in 1991.
US-Korea · South China Morning Post
Death of South Korean 'torture expert' reopens authoritarian era wounds
Lee Geun-an, a former intelligence officer known as South Korea's 'torture master' during the authoritarian era, died at age 88, reopening national debates about transitional justice. Lee gained notoriety for brutal interrogation methods during the Park Chung-hee dictatorship, particularly against pro-democracy activists in the 1960s-70s. His death without facing full accountability highlights South Korea's incomplete reckoning with its authoritarian past.
US-Korea · Yonhap English
S. Korea likely to co-sponsor U.N. resolution on N.K. human rights
South Korea will likely join as a co-sponsor of this year's UN resolution on North Korean human rights, reversing the previous administration's policy of non-participation. The move reflects the Lee Jae-myung government's harder line on DPRK human rights issues, though final confirmation awaits as consultations with UN member states continue ahead of the General Assembly vote expected in autumn.
DPRK · 뉴스1
Remembering Cheonan: 16 years after North Korean torpedo attack
March 26 marked the 16th anniversary of the ROKS Cheonan sinking that killed 46 South Korean sailors, an attack North Korea continues to deny despite international investigation findings. The incident remains a flashpoint in inter-Korean relations as families of victims and veterans held memorial services across South Korea. The attack fundamentally shifted ROK defense posture and alliance coordination.
Japan-Korea · Korea Times
ROK-Japan coast guards continue joint rescue for Indonesian crewman
South Korean and Japanese Coast Guards conducted a second day of joint rescue operations searching for an Indonesian crewman who fell into waters 195km northeast of Dokdo. A 5,000-ton ROK vessel and 1,250-ton Japanese vessel coordinated search efforts alongside civilian fishing boats, demonstrating operational cooperation despite the sensitive location near disputed islets.

North Korean media split focus between Kim's formal reelection at the SPA and hosting Belarus president, using both to project regime stability.
