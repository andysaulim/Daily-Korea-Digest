DPRK

Ex-President Yoon sentenced to 30 yrs in prison in drone infiltration case

A Seoul court on Friday sentenced former President Yoon Suk-yeol to 30 years in prison for ordering military drones to infiltrate North Korean airspace and drop propaganda leaflets, finding him guilty of violating national security laws and endangering inter-Korean relations. The court ruled that Yoon's authorization of at least five drone missions between 2024-2025 constituted acts that could have triggered military conflict on the Korean Peninsula. Yoon becomes the third former South Korean president to receive a lengthy prison sentence after leaving office, following Park Geun-hye and Lee Myung-bak.

So what: Affects inter-Korean confidence-building measures ahead of potential diplomatic engagement

Business

Seoul stocks spike over 4 pct on hopes for end to Mideast crisis

Seoul stocks surged more than 4 percent Friday to close at 8,123.62, as investors bet on potential de-escalation in Middle East tensions following reports of renewed US-Iran nuclear negotiations. Foreign investors net purchased 894.3 billion won ($588 million) worth of Korean shares, the largest single-day foreign buying since January, while the Korean won strengthened 2.19 percent against the dollar to 1,520.45. The rally was led by technology and defense stocks, with Samsung Electronics gaining 5.2 percent and Hanwha Aerospace jumping 8.1 percent on expectations of reduced geopolitical risk premiums.

S. Korea, Britain sign revised nuclear cooperation protocol

South Korea and Britain signed a revised nuclear cooperation protocol on Friday to expand bilateral partnership in peaceful nuclear technology development and small modular reactor deployment. The agreement updates the 1991 nuclear cooperation pact to include provisions for joint research on next-generation reactors and streamlined technology transfer procedures for civilian nuclear projects. Britain seeks Korean expertise for its nuclear power plant renewal program, while Korean firms eye opportunities in the UK's planned construction of up to eight new reactors by 2050.

DPRK · Yonhap English
Half of S. Korea's young adults view N. Korea with hostility: poll
Nearly half of South Koreans in their 20s and 30s view North Korea as hostile, marking a significant shift in generational attitudes toward inter-Korean relations, according to a new survey. The poll found 49% of respondents aged 20-39 described North Korea as an 'enemy' or 'hostile state,' up from 35% just two years ago. The hardening attitudes reflect growing skepticism among younger Koreans about engagement with Pyongyang following repeated provocations and the regime's deepening military ties with Russia.
US-Korea · Yonhap English
UAE sends military planes to transport Cheongung missile systems
The United Arab Emirates has dispatched military transport aircraft to South Korea to collect Cheongung-II medium-range surface-to-air missile systems under a $3.5 billion defense deal signed in 2022. Multiple UAE Air Force C-17 Globemaster cargo planes arrived at a South Korean air base this week to transport the first batch of missile interceptors and associated radar systems. The Cheongung-II, also known as M-SAM, provides capabilities similar to the US Patriot system and represents South Korea's largest defense export contract to date.
