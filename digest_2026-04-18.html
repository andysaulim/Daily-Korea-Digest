Top Stories

Lee calls for reopening of Strait of Hormuz, pledges support for free navigation

President Lee Jae-myung on Friday called for the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and pledged South Korea's support for freedom of navigation in the critical waterway. The statement comes amid heightened Middle East tensions following Iran's closure of the strait last week, threatening global oil supplies. South Korea imports approximately 70% of its crude oil through the Hormuz passage.

So what: Directly affects Korea's energy security and upcoming bilateral energy talks with Gulf states.

Finance minister says won stabilizing against dollar, hopes for market alignment

Finance Minister Koo Yun-cheol said Friday the Korean won is stabilizing against the dollar and expressed hopes for alignment with market expectations during meetings in Washington. The won has gained 1.11% this week to 1,465.68 per dollar, recovering from March lows amid improved export data and foreign investment inflows.

So what: Affects BOK's monetary policy considerations ahead of April 24 rate decision.

The Nomination of Michelle Steel and How the Senate Confirms an Ambassador

Korea Economic Institute of America examines the nomination of Representative Michelle Steel as U.S. Ambassador to South Korea and the Senate confirmation process. The analysis reviews Steel's Korean-American background, congressional experience on foreign affairs committees, and potential confirmation timeline given current Senate dynamics and the critical nature of the Seoul posting.

So what: Steel's confirmation would fill the ambassadorial vacancy ahead of key alliance negotiations.
