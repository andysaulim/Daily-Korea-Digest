North Korean hackers steal $36M from blockchain authentication platform

North Korean cybercriminals stole up to $36 million in cryptocurrency from identity verification service Humanity Protocol on June 8, moving 141.18 million Humanity tokens through phishing attacks. The heist continues Pyongyang's targeting of decentralized finance platforms, with blockchain analytics firms tracking over $3 billion stolen by DPRK hackers since 2017. The stolen funds typically support weapons programs and regime finances outside the formal banking system.

So what: Demonstrates DPRK's sustained cyber revenue generation despite international sanctions
