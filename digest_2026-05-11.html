Top Stories

KOSPI surges to new peak of above 7,800 on chip gains

South Korean stocks closed at a new record high Monday, with the benchmark KOSPI jumping 18.54 points to 7,822.24 as semiconductor shares led gains. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix drove the rally amid optimism over AI chip demand, while investors shrugged off renewed tensions over the U.S.-Iran nuclear deal and the attack on a Korean vessel in the Strait of Hormuz.

Yonhap English

Cheong Wa Dae says any attack on civilian ships cannot be tolerated

The presidential office said Monday that any attacks on civilian vessels in international waters cannot be tolerated, responding to the explosion aboard a Korean-flagged ship in the Strait of Hormuz. Seoul confirmed the blast was caused by an external strike but stopped short of identifying the perpetrator, with the opposition People Power Party criticizing the government for not naming Iran despite intelligence assessments.

So what: Complicates ROK energy security calculations ahead of May 14-15 Trump-Xi summit

Yonhap English

North Korean soldiers march in Moscow parade

North Korean military personnel marched in Russia's Victory Day parade in Moscow, marking their first participation in the annual event and signaling deepening military ties. Kim Jong Un sent a message to Putin reaffirming commitment to expanding bilateral cooperation, as the two countries continue weapons transfers and prepare for potential technology exchanges in missile and space programs.

Analysis of North Korea's recent constitutional amendments reveals Kim Jong Un has been granted formal powers that vastly exceed those held by his father and grandfather. China's economic leverage over South Korea has declined but Beijing retains critical supply chain chokepoints in semiconductors and batteries
CSIS analysis examines the evolving strategic value of China to South Korea's economy and security.
