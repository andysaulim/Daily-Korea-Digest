Top Stories

KOSPI surges to new peak of above 7,800 on chip gains despite Middle East woes

South Korean stocks closed at a record high Monday with the KOSPI surging 18.54 percent to 7,822.24 points, driven by semiconductor rallies amid AI demand. The gains came despite renewed concerns over US-Iran peace deal breakdown and Middle East tensions threatening global trade routes. Korea Exchange activated trading circuit breakers during the session as buying volume spiked across technology sectors.

So what: Record levels reflect investor confidence in Korean tech leadership but create bubble risks if Middle East crisis escalates

Bessent says will visit Seoul ahead of Trump-Xi summit

U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent plans to visit Seoul before the Trump-Xi summit scheduled for May 14-15 in Beijing, according to the Treasury Department. The visit will focus on US-Korea trade relations and coordination ahead of the bilateral summit between Presidents Trump and Xi Jinping. Bessent's Seoul stop signals the administration's priority on alliance coordination as it manages China relations.

So what: Directly affects US-Korea trade policy alignment ahead of the May 14-15 Trump-Xi Beijing summit

Yonhap English

Defense chiefs of S. Korea, U.S. set to hold talks amid OPCON transfer push

The top defense chiefs of South Korea and the United States are scheduled to hold talks this week amid ongoing discussions over wartime operational control transfer and the Hormuz ship incident. The meeting will address alliance modernization efforts and Seoul's push to assume wartime command of combined forces from Washington. Recent tensions in the Strait of Hormuz have added urgency to defense cooperation discussions.

So what: Critical for OPCON timeline and alliance posture as Middle East tensions test combined defense capabilities

N. Korea marches in Russia's Victory Day parade in show of military bond
The unification ministry confirmed North Korean military personnel participated in Russia's Victory Day parade, demonstrating deepening military cooperation between Pyongyang and Moscow. The attack escalates tensions as Middle East conflicts threaten global trade routes critical to Korean exports.

Exports up 43.7 pct during first 10 days of May on robust chip demand
South Korea's exports jumped 43.7 percent from a year earlier during the first 10 days of May, driven by strong semiconductor demand globally. Semiconductor exports have been the primary driver of Korea's trade surplus expansion in 2026.
