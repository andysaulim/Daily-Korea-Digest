Top Stories

Lee visits Jeju Air crash site, orders thorough reinvestigation

President Lee Jae-myung made his first visit to the Jeju Air crash site in Muan, ordering a complete reinvestigation after the initial probe was criticized for significant oversights. The December 2025 crash killed 179 people when flight 2216 from Bangkok crashed during landing. Lee directed authorities to examine all factors including bird strikes, landing gear failure, and runway design flaws that may have contributed to South Korea's deadliest aviation disaster.

Yonhap English

Unification white paper pivots to 'two-state' peaceful coexistence

Seoul's 2026 unification white paper formally shifted from pursuing immediate reunification to establishing peaceful coexistence between two Korean states. The Lee administration's policy pivot acknowledges North Korea as a separate state rather than rebel-occupied territory, marking the most significant doctrinal change since democratization. The white paper emphasizes reducing military tensions and expanding humanitarian cooperation while deferring reunification goals to future generations.

Yonhap English

Lee links 1980 democracy movement to thwarting 2024 martial law

President Lee Jae-myung commemorated the 46th anniversary of the May 18 Gwangju Democratic Uprising by drawing direct parallels to the December 2024 martial law attempt. Speaking at the national cemetery, Lee stated the solidarity that defeated authoritarian forces in 1980 was reborn when citizens blocked President Yoon's martial law declaration. Six former intelligence officials were booked Monday for their alleged roles in the 2024 insurrection attempt, as investigations continue.
