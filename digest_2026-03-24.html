Top Stories

N. Korea's Kim officially recognizes S. Korea as 'most hostile' nation

Kim Jong Un formally recognized South Korea as the 'most hostile state' in his policy speech at the Supreme People's Assembly, marking the first official implementation of his January 2024 declaration to abandon unification. The designation appeared in multiple contexts including constitutional amendments and new state policy directives, fundamentally altering the legal framework for inter-Korean relations.

So what: Affects all future inter-Korean agreements and humanitarian cooperation frameworks

North Korea's Kim Jong Un hits out at US 'aggression' amid Iran war

Kim Jong Un condemned US military actions in the Middle East as 'reckless aggression' during his Supreme People's Assembly speech, explicitly linking North Korea's nuclear program to deterring similar US intervention. The Financial Times reports Kim cited the Iran conflict as validation of North Korea's nuclear weapons policy, using unusually direct language about US military intentions.

So what: Signals potential DPRK escalation timing linked to US Middle East engagement

S. Korean won rebounds from 17-yr low on Middle East de-escalation hopes

The won strengthened to 1,500.56 per dollar after touching 1,520 in early trading, its weakest level since the 2009 financial crisis, as markets anticipated potential diplomatic progress in the Middle East conflict. Currency dealers cited intervention signals from Korean authorities and coordinated G7 statements on energy market stability as factors supporting the rebound.

So what: BOK expected to maintain accommodative stance at March 28 rate decision

Energy · Yonhap English
Qatar declares force majeure on LNG contracts with S. Korea amid Mideast crisis
Qatar's state energy firm invoked force majeure on liquefied natural gas contracts with South Korea, China, Italy and Belgium, citing operational disruptions from the expanding Middle East conflict. The declaration threatens Korea's energy security as Qatar supplies approximately 20% of Korea's LNG imports, prompting urgent diplomatic outreach to alternative suppliers including Oman and Australia.
NK-Russia-China · Yonhap English
Belarus leader Lukashenko to visit N. Korea: KCNA
Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko will visit North Korea at Kim Jong Un's invitation, KCNA reported Tuesday, marking the first visit by a European leader since the pandemic. The visit signals expanding North Korea's diplomatic outreach beyond Russia and China, potentially offering new channels for sanctions evasion through Belarus's role as Russia's key ally.
DPRK · Yonhap English
N. Korea to introduce police system in bid to project normal state image
Kim Jong Un unveiled plans to establish a formal police system separate from the existing Public Security Ministry, part of broader efforts to project North Korea as a normal state. The move, announced at the Supreme People's Assembly, represents a significant shift from the traditional security apparatus structure that has blurred lines between police and political control functions.
DPRK · Diplomat
North Korea enshrines nuclear weapons as necessity for future generations
Kim Jong Un's policy speech at the 15th Supreme People's Assembly definitively declared nuclear weapons a permanent necessity for regime survival, rejecting any future denuclearization pathway. The formulation specifically invoked 'future generations,' signaling that nuclear possession is now viewed as non-negotiable inheritance for Kim's daughter and successor generation.

The Iran war's expansion threatens Korea's energy security more than any crisis since 1973, requiring immediate diversification beyond Middle Eastern suppliers
