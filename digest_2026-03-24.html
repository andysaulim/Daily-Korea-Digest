Bangladeshi entrepreneur bridges Korea-Bangladesh through shrimp waste tech
Andrua Haque, a Global Korea Scholarship alumnus pursuing a master's at Seoul National University, co-founded MFM, a circular food tech startup converting shrimp byproducts from Bangladesh's tiger shrimp industry into premium pet food and eco-friendly fertilizer. Cabinet approves prosecution reform bills to dismantle current system
The Cabinet approved two prosecution reform bills Tuesday that would dismantle the current prosecution service in October, 78 years after its establishment, creating separate agencies for investigation and indictment. N. Korea to introduce police system to project itself as normal state
North Korean leader Kim Jong Un unveiled a plan to introduce a police system, marking the first time the country will have a formal police force since its founding. The move appears aimed at projecting North Korea as a normal state while strengthening social control mechanisms beyond the existing security apparatus.
Business · Korea Times
TB cases hit record high among elderly despite overall decline
Tuberculosis cases in Korea fell for the 14th consecutive year to 17,070 in 2025, down 4.9% year-on-year, but infections among those 65 and older rose 1.3% to 10,669 cases, accounting for 62.5% of all cases. The structural shift reflects Korea's aging demographics and poses challenges for public health infrastructure designed for younger populations.
