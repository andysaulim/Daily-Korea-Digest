KOSPI crashes 9%, circuit breaker triggered, on tech losses and Middle East tensions

Seoul shares plunged 9 percent on Monday, prompting KRX to activate both a sell-side sidecar and a full circuit breaker — the most severe market intervention mechanism — as investors sold technology shares amid renewed Middle East uncertainty. The financial watchdog chief urged better consumer protection amid the volatility. South Korea's export surge of 53.9% in the first 10 days of July, driven by chips, provided a stark contrast with the equity selloff.

So what — BOK is widely expected to raise its benchmark rate to curb mounting inflation pressure at its next meeting, adding a monetary tightening decision to an already stressed equity market environment.
