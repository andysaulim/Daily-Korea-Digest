Top Stories

South Korea hit by steepest stocks selloff since 2008, currency tumbles

Korean stocks suffered their worst single-day decline since the 2008 financial crisis as the KOSPI index plunged 7.1% to 5,052.46, nearing bear market territory. The Korean won weakened past 1,525 per dollar, its lowest level in years, as foreign investors pulled capital amid escalating Iran war risks. Trading volume hit record highs with institutional selling overwhelming retail buying attempts.

So what: Emergency market stabilization measures likely at BOK's Apr 24 monetary policy meeting

Pattern: 2008 financial crisis saw KOSPI drop 10.6% in single day

Reuters Korea

National Assembly ratifies trade pact with UAE

The National Assembly ratified Korea's first Middle Eastern free trade agreement with the UAE, lifting tariffs on over 90% of goods traded between the two countries over the next 10 years. The Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement, signed in May 2024 after three years of negotiations, benefits Korean foodstuffs, cosmetics, and defense exports while eliminating tariffs on UAE crude imports.

So what: Opens defense export opportunities as Korea seeks Middle East market expansion

Korea Times

Tass news agency's delegation in Pyongyang

A Russian state media delegation from TASS arrived in Pyongyang, according to North Korean state media reports monitored by Yonhap. The visit comes amid deepening NK-Russia cooperation across military, economic, and information spheres. TASS has increasingly amplified North Korean messaging on nuclear deterrence and anti-Western positions.

So what: Media cooperation typically precedes larger diplomatic or military exchanges

Energy · Korea Times
Lee to deliver budget speech Thursday to outline extra budget
President Lee Jae-myung will deliver a budget address Thursday outlining the 26.2 trillion won ($17.1 billion) emergency budget approved by Cabinet. The proposal focuses on easing fuel price impacts from Middle East tensions and includes cash handouts for 70% of the population. Lee will call for bipartisan support to ensure swift National Assembly passage.
Energy · Reuters Korea
South Korea proposes $17.3 billion extra budget to mitigate Middle East shock
South Korea's government proposed a 26.2 trillion won ($17.3 billion) supplementary budget to cushion economic impacts from the Middle East conflict. The budget includes measures to stabilize fuel prices, support households through direct cash transfers, and bolster strategic energy reserves. Finance ministry officials said the package targets three key areas: energy price response, economic stabilization, and public livelihood support.
US-Korea · Korea Times
US military squadron in Alaska joins live-fire drills in Korea
A U.S. military squadron from Alaska conducted live-fire exercises with Korean forces at Yeongpyeong Training Area, demonstrating rapid deployment capabilities from the Arctic to the Korean Peninsula. The drills involved paratroopers from the 1st Squadron, 40th Cavalry Regiment, 11th Airborne Division and highlighted the U.S. Army's transformation initiative to optimize warfighting readiness. Lt. Col. Craig Nelson noted this was a rare training opportunity at a facility not often accessible to U.S. forces.
NK-Russia-China · NKPro
North Korean ship docks at southern Chinese port in likely sanctions breach
The North Korea-flagged Puyun 6 made a rare voyage to China's Port of Fangcheng in the Gulf of Tonkin, analysis of maritime data shows, in what experts called a clear violation of UN sanctions. The vessel, newly acquired by North Korea, docked at a port known for handling coal and iron shipments. The trip underscores China's continued disregard for DPRK sanctions enforcement despite international pressure.
DPRK · Dong-A English
North Korea recasts Kim Ju Ae's public role
North Korean state media appears to be adjusting Kim Ju Ae's public positioning, shifting from emphasizing her as a potential successor to highlighting her role as a symbol of the next generation. Recent appearances show her driving a tank in military exercises rather than attending high-level political events. Analysts note this recalibration may reflect internal debates about succession planning within the Kim regime.
