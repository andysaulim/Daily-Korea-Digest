Top Stories

Seoul spy agency says it's fair to view Kim Jong Un's daughter as his heir

South Korea's National Intelligence Service assessed it is 'appropriate to regard' North Korean leader Kim Jong Un's daughter Ju-ae as his successor during closed-door briefing to lawmakers Monday. NIS analysis marks shift from earlier assessments treating her public appearances as regime legitimacy messaging. Agency cited her increasing centrality in military imagery and expanding public role since first appearance in November 2022.

So what: Affects succession planning assessments ahead of Apr 15 Day of the Sun celebrations

North Korea building giant wholesale complex with Chinese help

North Korean and Chinese companies are constructing sprawling wholesale complex in Pyongyang set to open later this year, according to Chinese businessman's Douyin video posted April 1. Complex represents latest sign of expanding economic ties between two countries as Chinese business presence in North Korea continues growing. Construction comes amid broader pattern of Chinese investment in North Korean commercial infrastructure.

Cabinet approves motion to amend parts of the Constitution

Cabinet approved motion Monday to amend Constitution following joint proposal by 187 lawmakers from ruling Democratic Party and five minor parties to tighten requirements for declaring martial law. President Lee Jae Myung required to post public notice of proposed amendment for at least 20 days before National Assembly vote. Constitutional amendment requires two-thirds majority in Assembly and national referendum to take effect.

Special counsel seeks 10-year prison term for ex-President Yoon
A special counsel team demanded a 10-year prison term for former President Yoon Suk Yeol during appeals trial for obstruction of justice case stemming from 2024 martial law imposition. The sentence request doubles the five-year term handed down by lower court in January. Yoon was convicted of obstructing investigators from detaining him and calling only select Cabinet members to review martial law plan.
US-Korea · Korea Times
Korean military launches annual Hwarang defense drills
Korean military kicked off monthlong annual defense drills in coordination with government, police and firefighting agencies to enhance integrated defense posture across regions. First segment of Hwarang drills began Monday for five-day run in Daegu and North Gyeongsang Province, continuing through November. This year's drills focus on training under emergency scenarios including drone attacks against energy facilities and key national infrastructure.
